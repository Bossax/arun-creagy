### 2. Immediate Objective: Participant Grouping Logic

The next task is to create the specific group lists for **6 breakout tables** (approx. 10 people per table). The design requires each group to function as a **"Mini-Ecosystem."**

**Grouping Constraints to Solve:**

1. **Role Balance**: Every table must have at least one **Data Producer** (e.g., TMD, GISTDA, NSO), one **Data User** (e.g., BMA, DPT, Banks), and one **Funder/Policy Setter** (e.g., NESDC, Budget Bureau).
    
2. **Sector Diversity**: Avoid clumping all "Water" agencies or all "Financial" agencies together. We need cross-pollination (e.g., a Bank rep talking to a Disaster Response rep).
    
3. **Seniority Balance**: Distribute high-level Directors and operational practitioners evenly. Too much seniority at one table might stifle the "Detail Cards"; too little might result in ideas that aren't institutionally feasible.
    
4. **DCCE Presence**: Each table needs one DCCE facilitator/notetaker who understands the 5 Themes but remains "tech-neutral."
