## 1. Section mandate from control files

From the benchmark and style pack, the expected functions of `2.2 ผังระบบ` are:

- Explain **why the chosen system components matter** for children and youth, especially orphans and vulnerable groups.
- Describe explicit **cause–effect relationships** between key nodes (e.g., household economy, learning pathways, welfare/protection, trust/safety, digital, environmental risk, community networks).
- Make visible a small number of **feedback loops and bottlenecks** that lock in or open up life chances.
- Build directly on `2.1.x` by turning signals and life-world trends into a **coherent system map**, not a second narrative of the same themes.
- Keep digital and climate as **important modifiers and stressors**, but not as new organising pillars beyond the team-origin spine.
- Prepare the reader for `2.2.1 การวิเคราะห์ผลกระทบไขว้` and later **baseline/scenario logic** in Chapter 3.

Section-function discipline from the style contract:

- The section must **show what connects to what** and **through which mechanism**, not only restate that “systems are linked”.
- Each paragraph should have one dominant job (e.g., define nodes, explain one loop, surface bottlenecks), with **actor-first and mechanism-first** prose.

## 3. Main gaps against section function and style contract

### Gap A — too much repeated narrative, not enough explicit system structure

- Large parts of the current text (especially under the **ความไม่สงบในพื้นที่** and digital paragraphs) rephrase earlier explanations from `2.1.x` rather than doing distinct system-mapping work.
- Comments inside the draft already flag this: _“นี่ไม่ใช้ผังระบบ ไม่มีการบอกว่าอะไรเชื่อมโยงกันอย่างไร ต้ิงเชื่อมกับระบบอื่นด้วย แถมเป็นการพูดซ้ำสิ่งที่พูดมาก่อนหน้าอีกรอบเฉยๆ”_.

**Implication for rewrite**

- The rewrite must **compress general descriptions** that belong to `2.1` and repurpose the space to:
  - name the main nodes explicitly and consistently (using style-pack terminology such as ระบบการเรียนรู้, ระบบเศรษฐกิจครัวเรือน, ระบบสวัสดิการและการคุ้มครอง, ระบบดิจิทัลและสภาพแวดล้อมออนไลน์, บริบทด้านความปลอดภัยและความไว้วางใจ, ระบบความสัมพันธ์ในชุมชน)
  - show **directional arrows** in prose (if X increases, Y changes via mechanism Z for children group W).

### Gap B — feedback loops are long-winded and partially duplicated

- Lines 239–252 attempt to explain three feedback loops but:
  - take up disproportionate space relative to earlier paragraphs;
  - repeat mechanisms already implied in the thematic sub-sections; and
  - still use abstract language such as _“ต้นทุนและความเชื่อมั่น”_ without immediately specifying whose cost and confidence, and in relation to which node.
- The internal comment suggests that these loops could be **condensed into a tighter synthesis** focusing on connection points between loops, rather than three semi-independent mini-essays.

**Implication for rewrite**

- Replace the long loop subsection with a **single, tight loop block** that:
  - names three loops (economy–learning, trust–access, digital–well-being/learning);
  - for each, gives **one actor-first mechanism sentence**; and
  - highlights the **shared connectors** (documentation, recognition, trusted intermediaries) instead of repeating full narratives.

### Gap C — missing explicit “Society & Quality of Life” node

- The draft comment at lines 232–237 notes the absence of a cluster for **Society & Quality of Life** (คุณภาพชีวิต ความสัมพันธ์ในครอบครัว การยอมรับความหลากหลาย), even though team signals include identity, inclusion/exclusion, and intra-family relations.
- Current 2.2 mentions trust and community, but **does not treat everyday quality of life, belonging, and family relational health as a distinct node** connecting systems.

**Implication for rewrite**

- Introduce a concise **social/quality-of-life cluster** that:
  - links family relations, acceptance of diversity, and safe learning/social spaces;
  - explains how this node both affects and is affected by economy, learning, welfare/protection, and security context.

### Gap D — digital and climate remain overweight relative to team anchors

- In line with the benchmark, digital and environmental risk are again given **full standalone paragraphs and long explanations**, while:
  - plural learning pathways;
  - Malay Muslim identity and culturally grounded learning;
  - local/halal/regional economy; and
  - concrete household care patterns
  are less visible in the main system narrative.

**Implication for rewrite**

- Rebalance weighting so that:
  - **household economy**, **learning pathways**, **welfare/protection and trust**, and **community/religious networks** carry most of the explanatory weight;
  - **digital** is written as a **cross-cutting modifier** of these nodes (learning continuity, protection channels, aspiration and work, risk exposure), not as a separate pillar; and
  - **environmental risk** is treated as a **stress test** for the mapped system (linking to wild cards in `2.1.3`), not as a new system on its own.

### Gap E — weak continuity with re-framed 2.1.2 life-world arc

- The gap analysis and synthesis spine for `2.1.2` reframe that section around three life trends: learning discontinuity, uneven care/protection, and identity-compatible/cross-border opportunity, with digital as modifier.
- Current `2.2` is not yet **visibly aligned** with that arc. It talks about economy, education, trust, digital, and environment, but:
  - does not pick up the concrete life trajectories (transition points, household patterns, Malaysia/halal routes) as nodes; and
  - does not make clear how the loops help explain the **trajectory stratification** flagged in `2.1.2` and in the baseline future.

**Implication for rewrite**

- Re-anchor node descriptions and loops using the **same backbone** as `2.1.2`:
  - economy–learning continuity;
  - care/protection and household pattern;
  - identity- and region-linked opportunity;
  - digital as amplifier.
- Make the closing paragraph of `2.2` explicitly show how these loops **generate the stratified trajectories** that Chapter 3 will extend into futures.

### Gap F — actor-first, mechanism-first discipline is applied unevenly

- Some paragraphs (e.g., the one on community and religious networks) already articulate clear mechanisms: when networks are strong, they mediate trust and access; when they are weak or under pressure, vulnerability rises.
- Other paragraphs open with **abstract system labels** (e.g., “ความไว้วางใจต่อสถาบันสาธารณะ” or “แพลตฟอร์มดิจิทัล”) and only later mention which children or families are affected and how.

**Implication for rewrite**

- Restructure paragraphs so that:
  - each opens with a **concrete actor or situation** (e.g., “เมื่อครอบครัวที่ดูแลเด็กกำพร้าต้องรับมือกับหนี้และรายได้ไม่แน่นอน…”);
  - mechanisms are spelled out as **A → B → C** with named nodes; and
  - abstract nouns (ความเปราะบาง ความไม่ต่อเนื่อง พลวัต) are used only after the concrete mechanism is visible.

## 4. Synthesis-spine decision for 2.2

Given:

- the complexity of the system map;
- the need to realign `2.2` with the re-framed `2.1.x` sections and the Chapter 3 baseline; and
- the requirement to **condense** feedback loops without losing clarity,

**Recommendation:** create a **compact synthesis spine** for `2.2 ผังระบบ` before rewriting, similar in spirit to `2.1.2` but lighter.

Minimum elements for the spine:

1. A short **section-mandate block** restating the job of `2.2` in one paragraph.
2. A paragraph-level **node and block structure**, e.g.:
   - Block 1: household economy and learning continuity (economy–education loop and its bottlenecks).
   - Block 2: welfare/protection, documentation, and trust (trust–access loop).
   - Block 3: community and religious networks, family and social quality-of-life node.
   - Block 4: digital as cross-cutting modifier.
   - Block 5: environmental risk as stress test across all loops.
3. A brief **feedback-loop synthesis** with **three named loops** and one paragraph that pulls them together.
4. A closing **bridge paragraph** that links `2.2` to:
   - `2.2.1` (cross-impact analysis) as “what happens when two nodes move together”; and
   - Chapter 3 (baseline and scenarios) as “what happens when these loops operate over time under different structural conditions”.

This spine will help keep the rewrite focused on section function, prevent drift into general narrative, and maintain continuity with the rest of Chapter 2.
