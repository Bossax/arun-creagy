## 2) Diagnosis – what is strong, what is weak, what is missing

### 2.1 Scenario axes (แกนตรรกะฉากทัศน์)

**Current formulation in v3-edited**

- Axes are explicitly defined in `3.3 แกนฉากทัศน์และชุดตัวบ่งชี้` as:
  - Learning system axis: from “ระบบการเรียนรู้แบบทางเดียวและการรับรองที่ยึดมาตรฐานส่วนกลาง” to “ระบบการเรียนรู้แบบหลากหลายที่เชื่อมต่อได้และการรับรองวุฒิการศึกษาหลายเส้นทาง”.
  - Economy axis: from “เศรษฐกิจยังเปราะบางและพึ่งพางานมูลค่าเพิ่มต่ำ” to “เศรษฐกิจยกระดับบนฐานทุนวัฒนธรรมและดิจิทัล (กระจายโอกาสมากขึ้น)”.
- The 2×2 matrix is clearly articulated and consistent with:
  - Quadrant analysis in [`Nationalism & Conservative Politics as Scenario Drivers.md`] (Single-path vs plural education × precarious vs upgraded economy).
  - Regional-integration framing in [`Geopolitics & Regional Integration.md`] and livelihood futures in [`Youth Livelihoods in the Deep South and Opportunity Structures.md`], [`วิถีทางเศรษฐกิจสำหรับเยาวชนในจังหวัดชายแดนภาคใต้ (พ.ศ. 2570–2590).md`].
- Scenario descriptions 1–4 match the four quadrants and are internally coherent with the axes.

**Strengths**

- Axes are **stable, well-anchored in evidence, and strongly aligned with the team’s narrative spine** (education pathways × economic opportunity structure).
- They translate the system map into a tractable 2D frame that is easy to remember and re-use in Chapter 4 (robustness tests, portfolio).
- The four quadrants correspond closely to the workshop enrichment quadrants in [`2025-09-30-Foresight 3 -  scenario enrichment.md`] and to the quadrant names in the nationalism and geopolitics source notes.

**Weaknesses / Gaps**

- Macro drivers that are analytically central in the sources (nationalism/conservative politics, securitisation, regional labour regimes, digital transformation, climate risk) are **mostly handled as background prose** in the baseline and scenarios, not as clearly mapped influences on the two axes.
  - E.g. nationalism is recognised in comments in `3.2 ภาพอนาคตฐาน` but not woven into the axis explanation in `3.3` as an explicit lock or lever on the learning-system axis.
  - Regional integration and Nusantara labour/halal dynamics from the geopolitics and livelihoods notes are referenced implicitly in scenario 2 and 4 but not systematically differentiated across all four worlds.
- Trust, safety, and identity/cultural capital – which the benchmark flags as core cross-cutting dimensions – are treated as **descriptors inside worlds**, not as structural conditions that can push worlds along the axes.

**Assessment**

- The **scenario axes themselves are stable enough and should be retained**. No change of axes is needed.
- What is missing is **explicit mapping from macro drivers and trust/identity conditions to positions on these axes**, so that later robustness testing can re-use the same geometry without inventing new dimensions.

### 2.2 Descriptor set (ชุดตัวบ่งชี้ฉากทัศน์)

**Current descriptor table (v3-edited)**

Eight shared descriptors are defined:

1. เส้นทางการเรียนรู้หลักและการเชื่อมต่อ  
2. สถานะทางเศรษฐกิจของครอบครัวและภาระการดูแล  
3. ตลาดงานและโอกาสรายได้ของเยาวชน  
4. ความปลอดภัย ความไว้วางใจ และพื้นที่คุ้มครองเด็ก  
5. ทุนวัฒนธรรมและอัตลักษณ์ในฐานะพื้นที่ยืน  
6. การใช้ทุนวัฒนธรรมและอัตลักษณ์ในเศรษฐกิจ  
7. ดิจิทัลและความปลอดภัยออนไลน์  
8. ชีวิตของเด็กกำพร้าและเด็กกลุ่มเปราะบาง

**Coverage against sources**

- System map and base_report emphasise:
  - Household economy, debt, and caregiving load.
  - Learning continuity, credential recognition, and system connectivity.
  - Trust, safety, and protection access.
  - Digital as both opportunity and risk.
  - Environmental risk and disasters as stress-tests on all loops.
- Nationalism, geopolitics, livelihoods, and identity notes add:
  - State nationalism/Thai-only curriculum, securitisation, and language policy as deep constraints on learning pathways.
  - Regional integration and Nusantara labour/halal markets as structural determinants of whether “upgraded economy” is rooted or extractive.
  - Youth identity-security and belonging as preconditions for using learning and labour opportunities.
  - Gendered constraints and Malaysia corridor feminisation.

**Strengths**

- The current eight-descriptor set **covers the right domains** and is readable and child-centred.
- It already distinguishes between “ทุนวัฒนธรรมในฐานะพื้นที่ยืน” and “การใช้ทุนวัฒนธรรมในเศรษฐกิจ”, which aligns with the identity and halal-economy sources.
- Descriptor 8 forces every scenario to answer explicitly “เด็กกำพร้าและเด็กกลุ่มเปราะบางยืนอยู่ตรงไหนของโลกนี้”.

**Weaknesses / Gaps**

- Some **descriptors are compound** and risk carrying too many jobs at once:
  - Descriptor 4 bundles safety, trust, and protection, though trust also operates as an enabling precondition for nearly all other descriptors.
  - Descriptor 7 stacks “digital as infrastructure” and “digital as risk/protection” in one line.
- **Environmental risk / climate stress** is not explicitly named in the descriptor set, even though it is central in Chapter 2 (`2.1.3 เหตุไม่คาดฝัน`, `2.2 ผังระบบ`) and in base_report.
- **Nationalism / governance model / securitisation** and **regional integration quality** are not exposed as reading lenses, even though they are critical for understanding how the same axes play out.
  - Currently these appear inside the narrative of certain scenarios (especially 1 and 2) but are not forced across all four.
- There is **no single descriptor for “identity-security and belonging”** even though this is foregrounded in the identity note and in the benchmark as a cross-cutting condition.

**Assessment**

- The **existing descriptor set is close but not yet sufficient** as the backbone for comparative reading and robustness tests.
- For Chapter 3 to function the way the benchmark intends, the descriptors should:
  - Be **slimmed to one job each**.
  - Make **identity-security, trust, and environmental risk** explicit.
  - Clarify the distinction between **structural axes** and **descriptors** (the current descriptors mix “axis-level” elements like economy/learning with “lens-level” elements like digital and identity).

### 2.3 Scenario names and narrative differentiation

**Current names in v3-edited**

1. กรอบเดียว โอกาสติดหล่ม  
2. โตแต่ไหลออก  
3. ยืนบนราก รายได้บาง  
4. นูซันตาราบนฐานราก

These emerged from team work and already carry strong metaphorical content.

**Strengths**

- Names are **internally consistent with the axes** and with the quadrant labels in the nationalism/geopolitics sources (Stagnant Border, Exclusive Hub, Cultural Autonomy Economic Stagnation, Patani Renaissance / Rooted Hub).
- Each name encodes at least one key tension:
  - 1: single-frame learning system + risk of getting stuck.
  - 2: upgraded economy that pulls people out.
  - 3: rooted identity with thin income.
  - 4: rooted identity plus Nusantara-connected upgraded economy.

**Weaknesses / Gaps**

- Names are **not yet pop-culture-friendly**; they rely on conceptual metaphors that require explanation.
- Scenario 1 and 2 names both evoke “ติดหล่ม/ไหลออก” but do not clearly encode learning vs economy logic for new readers.
- Scenario 4 “นูซันตาราบนฐานราก” is close to the Rooted-Hub/Nusantara Oasis narrative in the sources but could be made more accessible.

**Assessment**

- Narrative differentiation across the four worlds is solid, but **names can be modernised** to support communication and recall while preserving the logic of each world.

### 2.4 Need for a dedicated synthesis spine before rewriting

Comparing v3-edited, the output report reference, and the benchmark for Chapter 3:

- Baseline (`3.2`) is already anchored in Chapter 2 but still carries TODO comments to bring in nationalism, conservative politics, AI, climate change, and regional labour/halal trends more explicitly.
- `3.3` (axes and descriptors) is structurally sound but needs descriptor refinement and better mapping to macro drivers.
- Scenario narratives (`3.3.x`) are reasonably rich but mix:
  - clearly evidence-backed patterns (from nationalism, livelihoods, geopolitics, identity notes), and
  - extrapolations that would benefit from a more explicit audit trail (e.g. specific uses of AI, some digital-labour details).
- Chapter 4 already depends on this logic for robustness and portfolio analysis.

Given this, **a synthesis spine is advisable before any rewriting**, with three purposes:

1. **Lock the scenario geometry**: restate axes, macro-driver relationships, and descriptor set in one internal note so future revisions do not re-open the basic geometry.
2. **Map evidence to each descriptor and world**: create an internal grid that shows which aspects of each scenario are strongly anchored in existing sources vs interpretive extrapolation.
3. **Pre-commit communication choices**: scenario names (TH + EN), one-line tags, and which contrasts to foreground when the chapter is tightened.

This planning note plays part of that role for axes/descriptors/names, but a separate short synthesis spine for Chapter 3 (analogous to the Chapter 2 spines already created) will still be useful when actually rewriting narrative prose.

## 3) Proposed refined descriptor set (updated)

### 3.1 Design principles

- Derive descriptor content **primarilyจากผังระบบที่ใช้อยู่แล้ว** (เศรษฐกิจครัวเรือน ระบบการเรียนรู้ ความปลอดภัย ความไว้วางใจ ระบบดิจิทัล และความเสี่ยงสิ่งแวดล้อม) และจาก **ชุดความไม่แน่นอนสำคัญที่สังเคราะห์ตอนท้ายของบทที่ 2**.
- จากนั้นค่อย **ปรับแต่งและเน้นน้ำหนัก** โดยใช้บันทึกเรื่องชาตินิยม/การเมืองอนุรักษนิยม ภูมิรัฐศาสตร์และการบูรณาการระดับภูมิภาค โครงสร้างอาชีพและรายได้ของเยาวชน และอัตลักษณ์/ทุนวัฒนธรรม เป็นเลนส์ช่วยจัดกลุ่มและตั้งคำถาม.
- Keep **one job per descriptor**; avoid over-bundling.
- Preserve child-centred and mechanism-first framing from the style pack.
- Ensure descriptors can **carry all four scenarios** without needing ad hoc additions.
- Make cross-cutting constraints **visible**: identity-security, trust, environmental risk, and digital.
- Keep the original eight as the starting point, but allow regrouping.
### 3.2 Recommended descriptors (for use across all four scenarios)

**Axis-linked descriptors**  
These speak directly to the two critical-uncertainty axes and should be foregrounded first in each scenario.

1. **เส้นทางการเรียนรู้และการรับรองวุฒิ**  
   - What main learning pathways exist (รัฐ, เอกชนสอนศาสนา, ปอเนาะ, ชุมชน, ออนไลน์ ฯลฯ).  
   - How easy it is for children and youth, especially orphans and vulnerable groups, to move between them.  
   - How learning in each path is recognised (เทียบโอน, รับรองวุฒิ, micro-credentials) in practice.

2. **สภาพทางเศรษฐกิจของครัวเรือนและภาระการดูแล**  
   - Income level and stability, debt, and cost of living.  
   - Care burdens (เด็กกำพร้า, ผู้สูงอายุ, คนเจ็บป่วย) and how these shape school-to-work choices.  
   - Whether staying in learning pathways is affordable in everyday life.

3. **ตลาดงานและเส้นทางรายได้ของเยาวชน**  
   - Types of work available (local, มาเลเซีย, เมืองใหญ่, ดิจิทัล).  
   - Entry points for youth with different credentials (formal, religious, community-based).  
   - Brain drain vs rooted, circular opportunities.

**Cross-cutting social and cultural descriptors**

4. **ความปลอดภัย ความไว้วางใจ และการเข้าถึงการคุ้มครอง**  
   - Everyday safety in physical and digital spaces.  
   - Trust between households/communities and institutions (โรงเรียน, หน่วยสวัสดิการ, หน่วยงานรัฐ, หน่วยงานด้านความมั่นคง).  
   - How easy it is to access welfare and protection without adding new risks.

5. **อัตลักษณ์ ทุนวัฒนธรรม และความรู้สึกเป็นส่วนหนึ่ง**  
   - How children and youth experience belonging (บ้าน, โรงเรียน, ชุมชน, รัฐ, Nusantara).  
   - The status of Malay language, Islamic education, and cultural practices in each world.  
   - Whether identity functions more as protection or as constraint in that scenario.

6. **การใช้ทุนวัฒนธรรมและภาษามาลายูในเศรษฐกิจ**  
   - How cultural capital (ภาษา, ฮาลาล, เครือข่ายข้ามพรมแดน) is converted into income and opportunity.  
   - Whether local youth are value creators in the halal/creative economy or peripheral labour.

**Technology, environment, and vulnerability descriptors**

7. **ระบบเทคโนโลยีดิจิทัล สื่อออนไลน์ และการคุ้มครองเด็ก**  
   - Access to devices, connectivity, and **practical** digital skills in everyday life.  
   - The role of digital platforms and online services in **learning, work, welfare delivery, and protection**.  
   - Online risks (misinformation, scams, surveillance/monitoring) and the presence or absence of **digital child-protection safeguards**.

8. **ปัจจัยเสี่ยงจากภัยธรรมชาติและความต่อเนื่องของชีวิตประจำวัน**  
   - Frequency and severity of climate-related events and local environmental risks.  
   - How these shocks interact with school continuity, household economy, and protection systems.  
   - Whether systems in each world can keep learning and care going through crises.

9. **ช่องทางการดูแลและเส้นทางชีวิตของเด็กกำพร้าและเด็กกลุ่มเปราะบาง**  
   - Where these children sit in the system in each world (ในสายตาของโรงเรียน หน่วยบริการ ตลาดงาน).  
   - Who notices and supports them during transitions and shocks.  
   - Whether they have realistic, dignified pathways to adulthood in that world.

### 3.3 Notes on implementation

- The report does not need to show all nine descriptors in a long table again; it can:
  - Keep a **slimmed shared table** (e.g. 6–8 items) in `3.3` for reader orientation.
  - Use the extended list above internally as a **checklist for drafting scenario prose**, ensuring that key tensions (identity-security, trust, environmental risk, digital) are not accidentally dropped in certain worlds.
- Environmental risk (descriptor 8) can be treated as a **stress-test lens** used selectively in each scenario rather than given equal weight everywhere; but it should be present enough that Chapter 4 can still test robustness under climate shocks.

## 5) Synthesis spine requirement

Based on the above diagnosis and the benchmark guidance for Chapter 3:

1. **Descriptor adequacy**  
   - The current eight-descriptor set is **not fully adequate**; it needs:
     - Rebalancing into single-purpose descriptors.
     - Explicit treatment of identity-security, trust, and environmental risk.
     - Clearer alignment with the two axes and with the macro-driver source notes.

2. **Need for a synthesis spine**  
   - A **dedicated synthesis spine for Chapter 3 is needed** before rewriting scenario prose.  
   - This chapter-level spine should, at minimum:
     - Restate the axes and refined descriptor set agreed for the four worlds.
     - Map each scenario to key macro drivers: nationalism/conservative politics, regional integration quality, youth livelihood structures, identity-security, digital, and climate.  
     - Summarise, in one table, the expected sign/direction for each descriptor in each world, marking which cells are strongly evidence-backed versus interpretive.

3. **How this planning note will be used**  
   - When switching to drafting mode, this note should be read alongside:
     - [`plans/foresight-report-writing-style-pack.md`] for prose behaviour.  
     - The upcoming `2026-04-04_chapter3-synthesis-spine-3.2-scenarios.md` (or equivalent) for world-by-world descriptor mapping.  
   - The main v4 draft [`ψ/lab/foresight-report-wrting/2026-04-04_foresight-2590-integrated-rewrite-v4.md`] should then be revised so that:
     - `3.2` uses the same macro-driver logic and explicitly shows how the baseline sits in the same coordinate system.  
     - `3.3` reflects the refined descriptor logic.  
     - `3.3.x` uses the new scenario names and systematically walks through the shared descriptor lenses, with children and vulnerable youth at the centre.
