## Main cross-impact clusters emerging from the team matrix

### 1. Violence - justice - identity cluster

The matrix suggests a strong reinforcing cluster around:

- `สถานการณ์ความรุนแรงในพื้นที่`
- `ความเป็นธรรมทางสังคม`
- `ความสุดโต่งทางอัตลักษณ์`

This is one of the clearest structural findings in the team output. Violence is not acting alone as a security event. It is tied to perceived fairness, relationships with institutions, and identity hardening. In turn, when justice is perceived as weak and identity extremism rises, violence-related conditions tend to intensify further.

**Narrative implication for the report:** this should be written as a social-political feedback cluster that shapes the conditions under which children and families decide whether to trust institutions, remain in learning spaces, or withdraw.

### 2. Household pressure - welfare - education access cluster

Another strong cluster centers on:

- `ภาระหนี้สินครัวเรือน`
- `การช่วยเหลือจากรัฐ สวัสดิการต่อเด็กกำพร้า`
- `การเข้าถึงการศึกษาของเด็กกำพร้า`

The strongest downstream outcome in the whole matrix is `การเข้าถึงการศึกษาของเด็กกำพร้า`. This suggests that access to education is where many pressures converge. Debt pressure, welfare conditions, violence, and education policy all push on this node.

**Narrative implication for the report:** the cross-impact section should treat orphan education access not as one issue among many, but as a strategic hinge where multiple social, economic, and institutional pressures meet.

### 3. Education-governance cluster

The matrix also gives strong weight to:

- `นโยบายการศึกษา`
- `การเข้าถึงการศึกษาของเด็กกำพร้า`
- `ความสุดโต่งทางอัตลักษณ์`
- `ความเป็นธรรมทางสังคม`

This suggests that education policy is not just an administrative variable. In the team worldview, it shapes legitimacy, inclusion, and the long-term social effects of how the system recognizes or excludes different pathways.

**Narrative implication for the report:** education policy should be presented as a cross-impact lever that affects belonging, future opportunity, and social tension, not only curriculum or certification mechanics.

### 4. Opportunity-structure cluster

The matrix includes:

- `ความหลากหลายของอาชีพในพื้นที่`
- `มุมมองนักลงทุนต่อพื้นที่`

But the matrix suggests these are **less primary** than the justice-violence-education cluster. In particular, `มุมมองนักลงทุนต่อพื้นที่` has the weakest driver score among the listed factors. This implies investor perception is better understood as a **downstream system reflection**, not a core driver.

**Narrative implication for the report:** the report should avoid over-centering investor sentiment. It can be kept as a secondary indicator of area confidence and opportunity, but not as one of the main cross-impact anchors.

### 5. Technology as enabling condition, not system center

`การเข้าถึงและใช้เทคโนโลยี - AI` matters in the matrix, but it does not dominate the structure. It appears as an important enabling or mediating factor rather than the main engine of system change.

**Narrative implication for the report:** digital should be integrated where it affects learning access, information reliability, and work efficiency, but it should not displace the stronger team-owned drivers.

## Proposed structure for a workbook-grounded rewrite of `2.2.1`

Explain that the team matrix shows how pressures in one area shift outcomes in others, especially for orphaned and vulnerable children. Keep this short.

### Paragraph 2 — cluster one: violence, social justice, identity

Show that violence is not only a security issue. It interacts strongly with fairness and identity polarization, and together these shape trust, inclusion, and institutional legitimacy.

### Paragraph 3 — cluster two: debt, welfare, education access

Show that household debt and uneven welfare support shape educational access directly. This is the clearest convergence point in the matrix and should be the centerpiece of the subsection.

### Paragraph 4 — cluster three: education policy as system lever

Show that policy on curriculum, certification, and educational institutions is a cross-impact lever with consequences for access, legitimacy, and social tension.

### Paragraph 5 — secondary conditions: jobs, investor confidence, technology

Treat occupational diversity, investor view, and technology as downstream or enabling conditions, not as the main spine.
