## Honest Feedback
1) **Command execution friction**: the hybrid shell context made it easy to pick the wrong tool (`mv` vs `move`, `ls` vs `dir`) and increased the cost of routine actions.

2) **Over-ambitious command batching**: trying to migrate many files in one chained command created failure modes that were hard to diagnose and prompted direct user pushback (“it command is too big”). Smaller, atomic moves were more reliable and more aligned with an audit-friendly workflow.

3) **Git hygiene at wrap-up**: staging failed due to a stray `nul` file, and even after removing it the session ended without a clean staged/committed boundary. The repo also contained many unrelated changes beyond WMO-NFCS, which increases the risk of mixing concerns and weakening “ledger traceability” at the git level.

## Lessons Learned
- The PM ecosystem must stay minimal and explicit: if a surface (like a risk registry) isn’t part of the canonical set, it should be superseded and its useful content mapped into the canonical ledgers.
- Canonical locations matter more than names: once `inbox_source/` becomes the intake root, all other references must converge there quickly to avoid link rot.
- In a mixed shell environment, operational success improves when commands are short, single-purpose, and verified by listing results immediately.
