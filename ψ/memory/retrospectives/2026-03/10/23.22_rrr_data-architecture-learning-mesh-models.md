## Lessons Learned
- Data mesh is best understood as a **responsibility architecture** (who owns what, and what the shared rules are), not as a specific technology choice.
- Federated governance works when standards are centralized but enforcement is encoded in self-serve platform capabilities (catalog + access + logging + quality).
- Conceptual models are not “documentation overhead”; they’re the semantic contract that makes later logical/physical work stable.
- Canonical models reduce integration complexity, but require versioning discipline to avoid becoming a bottleneck.
- Medallion layers clarify data lifecycle and quality expectations; they can coexist with domain ownership (mesh).
