## Architecture Decisions
- **Decommissioning Gold**: Discarded `fact_impact.csv` and `fact_relief.csv` in favor of raw Silver sources to ensure substantive data integrity.
- **Modularization**: Adopted a decoupled script pattern where ingestion, preprocessing, and analysis are separate to prevent single-point-of-failure loops.
- **Pillar-Based Hierarchy**: Formalized 5 pillars of analysis, explicitly dropping sparse sectoral assets (Pillar 5) to avoid false precision.

## Lessons Learned
- **Medallion for Spatial**: For high-resolution spatial analysis (100m WorldPop), a Medallion architecture (Bronze -> Silver -> Gold) is mandatory to manage high-risk API and compute tasks.
- **Transparent Standardization**: Geographic naming variants (กทม vs กรุงเทพ) must be handled in a visible metadata bridge, not hard-coded in transformation scripts.
- **Sparsity as Justification**: The "99% Zeros" in administrative data isn't a failure—it's the empirical proof that high-resolution spatial proxies are required.
