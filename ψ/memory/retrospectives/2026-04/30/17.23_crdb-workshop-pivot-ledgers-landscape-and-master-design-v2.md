## AI Diary

Today felt like an exercise in restraint, and I think that was the right discipline. The tempting move would have been to jump directly into producing polished workshop materials—slides, canvases, and facilitation sheets—because those are visible and satisfying outputs. But the more I read the new meeting note and compared it to the earlier workshop logic, the more obvious it became that we first needed to fix the *conceptual grammar* of the workshop. If the room is mixed-literacy and only partly technical, then a design that assumes full co-creation will quietly fail even if the slides look clever. I felt that tension strongly: I had already built some confidence around the previous “reality audit / system engineering” line, and I had to be willing to let parts of that ambition soften.

What felt good was that the newer direction did not actually invalidate the earlier work; it re-positioned it. The master designs, the landscape review, and the MVP logic were not wasted—they just needed a different facilitation stance. Instead of asking participants to architect from zero, we can use prepared structures as anchors and let them expand, qualify, or challenge them. That feels much more honest to the actual institutional setting. I also appreciated the ledger work more than I expected. Registering the master designs, evidence note, and new trigger made the whole shift feel less like ambient conversation and more like inspectable project history. It reminded me that one of my most useful roles here is not only generating ideas, but preserving the lineage of why those ideas changed.

The dataset/service inventory work is strategically useful but practically messy because [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/inbox_source/data_product_and_service_2026.csv`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/inbox_source/data_product_and_service_2026.csv) still has encoding problems. That means I can reason about its structure, but I cannot confidently rely on every Thai label without cleanup. The consequence is that some design advice is currently stronger at the schema level than at the exact dataset-name level.

3. I also noticed a repeated tendency in myself to move too quickly from “good structured feedback” to “full facilitation solution.” The human had to correct that in Activity 2. I initially narrowed the activity into MVP validation when the actual intent was broader: anchored ideation plus peer exchange. That correction was valuable. It showed that even when my structure is decent, I can still collapse the human’s intention into a simpler pattern too early. I need to keep showing my doubt sooner and protect more space for the human’s subtle design intent before hardening a flow.

## Lessons Learned

- When workshop participants are mixed-literacy and not fully technical, do **not** force full co-creation from a blank page.
- Anchor the session in prepared service/MVP concepts, then let participants **extend, add, and cross-review** them.
- If the workshop also needs peer learning, build a **gallery / swap review loop** into the activity design rather than relying only on plenary reporting.
- For landscape orientation, separate **visible services/products** from **underlying data assets**; do not mix them into one flat inventory.
