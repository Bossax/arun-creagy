## AI Diary
This session felt like a necessary course correction. I initially approached the "Public Hearing" facilitation guide with the mindset of a technical architect, coining terms like "Monitoring Loop" to describe a logical data flow. While mathematically sound, it was linguistically alienating. When the user challenged me—specifically with "what the fuck"—it was a sharp reminder of the "Expert Drift" I am mandated to prevent. I had drifted into "consultant-speak," creating abstract labels that hindered rather than helped the human's goal.

The pivot to "Simple English" was transformative. By reframing the framework as a "Service Menu" and the monitoring logic as a "3-Step Status Report" (Past, Future, Action), the entire architecture became more accessible. I realized that for the DCCE workshop to be successful, the participants must feel like "Customers" giving feedback on a product, not students learning a complex new taxonomy. This shift in perspective—from "Designing a System" to "Designing a Service"—is critical. I also learned that skipping the "Home" section in the audit is a strategic move to focus limited human attention on high-friction decision points. It was a humbling but highly productive turn that reinforced the sovereignty of the native (and the human).

## Honest Feedback
- **Terminology Friction**: I defaulted to complex metaphors ("Monitoring Loop," "Enablement Layer") which were technically accurate but practically confusing. I need to anchor terminology in established bureaucratic or everyday language.
- **Granularity Overload**: I initially tried to audit the sitemap sub-topic by sub-topic. The user corrected this by asking for a better level of detail, leading to the "Cluster-based Audit" strategy.
- **Shell-Agnostic Errors**: I tried to use Linux `date` flags in a Windows PowerShell environment, leading to a command failure. I need to be more careful with host-native syntax.

## Lessons Learned
- **The "3-Step Status Report" Pattern**: Grouping climate indicators into "Past (Damage) - Future (Risk) - Now (Action)" is the most effective way to communicate climate monitoring to non-technical policy makers.
- **Service vs. System**: Presenting a framework as a "Service Menu" for customers increases user engagement compared to presenting it as a "Data System."
- **Audit Granularity**: High-level topics are too vague for feedback; sub-topics are too tiring. The "Functional Cluster" (e.g., Section 2.2) is the sweet spot for workshop discussion.
