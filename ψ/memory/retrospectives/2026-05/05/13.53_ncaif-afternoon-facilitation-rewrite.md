## AI Diary (first-person)

I felt the main tension of this session was a recurring ambiguity: whether the afternoon workshop should be treated as a governance-and-decision capture forum or as a user-facing website requirements capture exercise. Early drafts of the redesign leaned toward “decision points” because the existing execution-gap notes naturally emphasize ownership, rails, and endorsement. But once you clarified governance must be handled internally by DCCE, the correct posture changed: the workshop needs to function as a clean intake mechanism for expectations and needs, not a pseudo-steering committee.

The practical relief was that the system already had strong anchors for how to behave: the master design v2 frames the workshop as validate + guided feedback, and the sitemap explicitly exposes at least one real open choice (province-profile framing). With those anchors, I could stop trying to over-engineer the afternoon and focus on producing artifacts you can immediately use: slide narrative guidance, Mentimeter prompts that force concrete answers, and poster activities that yield a backlog rather than scattered opinions. I also noticed that I had to repeatedly guard against mixing “platform architecture” language into a “website expectations” session—those are related, but if I blur them, the participants will either disengage or we’ll capture unusable abstract feedback.

## Honest Feedback (3 friction points)

1) **Tooling vs truth friction**: the requirement to always use tools is workable, but it adds overhead when the real work is conceptual (facilitation and prompts). I had to be deliberate about not letting tool mechanics drive the design.

2) **MVP placement ambiguity**: notes implied MVPs were an afternoon anchor, but the master design shows MVP Activity 2 is in the morning. Resolving this mattered; otherwise the afternoon guide would conflict with the frozen workshop logic.

3) **Oracle sync gap**: the `/rrr` flow expects `arra_learn` for Oracle sync, but this environment doesn’t expose that command in PowerShell. That means the “sync” step must be recorded as incomplete unless a separate Oracle DB interface is available.

## Lessons Learned

- When governance is explicitly out of scope for a workshop session, convert “decision prompts” into “expectation prompts” that still produce implementation-ready outputs (tasks, trust blocks, missing pages, wording fixes) without forcing the room to assign owners or decision rights.
- Keep MVP/service concepts in the morning (where they already have worksheets and peer review) and use the afternoon to harvest concrete website/content requirements and language clarity issues.
