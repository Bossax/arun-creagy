## AI Diary (first-person)

I felt the session move from “we have a plan” to “we have decision-ready artifacts” once I treated the missing links and file-path mismatches as a signal: the plan’s intent was right, but the concrete anchors (which interview note, which strategy file) needed to be made explicit. After that, the workshop note became a set of constraints I could actually design for—especially the ~1 week lag in post-event data, the confusion about spatial units, and the multi-hazard/cascading-impacts literacy gap.

What surprised me is how quickly governance becomes practical when it’s framed as: “what do we need to safely publish Phase 1 products?” Boundary versioning and an event/impact schema aren’t abstract governance; they are prerequisites to prevent misleading outputs. I also noticed that the “hybrid NCAIF” idea (thematic backbone with a light persona entry) is less about fancy UX and more about preventing users from getting lost when climate literacy varies.

Momentum-wise, being on day 2 of an active streak helped: the work felt like a continuation of prior CDM/governance thinking, but with a sharper delivery lens.

## Honest Feedback

1) The plan relied on Obsidian-style wikilinks (e.g., `[[Interview result]]`, `[[Feature-Driven Data Governance Strategy]]`) that weren’t resolvable to real file paths. This caused avoidable friction and made it too easy to pull the wrong “source of truth.”

2) Some meta/template anchors are empty (notably [`src/00_Meta/_Meta.md`](src/00_Meta/_Meta.md:1) and [`src/00_Meta/template/template.md`](src/00_Meta/template/template.md:1)). When a plan references templates/workflows and the referenced file is blank, it undermines the reliability of the workflow.

3) The “interview result” proxy used here is short and high-level. It’s good enough to surface key constraints, but too thin to fully justify user-journey design choices; a stronger evidence base (more transcripts/notes) will be needed for FGD2 confidence.

## Lessons Learned

- Phase 1 NCAIF design must explicitly encode *event data reality* (lags, uncertainty, schema) and *boundary reality* (multiple spatial systems + crosswalks), or the portal will create false certainty.
- A hybrid navigation model can be justified as a climate-literacy accommodation, but the thematic taxonomy should remain the stable backbone.
- “Feature-driven governance” becomes concrete when it is packaged into repeatable work packages (dataset onboarding, product release, minimal quality checks, legal/sharing readiness).

## Next Steps

- Expand the “interview result” evidence base beyond a single workshop note (collect more interviews/transcripts).
- Use the FGD2 plan to capture decisions D1–D5 and update Phase 1 scope + CDM entity priorities.
- Draft (or adopt) a minimal event/impact reporting schema and boundary crosswalk rules for Phase 1 publishing.
