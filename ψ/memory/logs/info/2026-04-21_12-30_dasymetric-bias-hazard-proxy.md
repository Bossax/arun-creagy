Applying dasymetric mapping to affected people or casualties from disaster events using population distribution introduces a systematic bias toward densely populated areas. In fact, the best proxy for disaster casualties would be the historical hazard maps (since if there is no hazard, there are no casualties). 

We need to figure out how to improve the dasymetric mapping logic for Pillar 1. We are waiting for DDPM updated statistics which I hope would give us more complete data of casualties and affected people at the granular levels we desire.
