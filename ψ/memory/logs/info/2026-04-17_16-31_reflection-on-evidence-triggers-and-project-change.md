# Reflection on Evidence, Triggers, and Project Change

In the CRDB project architecture, the transition from new information to project evolution follows a disciplined, traceable pipeline that ensures "nothing is deleted" and every pivot is grounded.

1.  **Source to Evidence (The Provenance):** New research (e.g., Copernicus CDS, CMS paradigms) is first registered in the **Evidence Registry (E-IDs)**. This establishes the artifact's existence and provenance without yet committing to a change.
2.  **Evidence to Trigger (The Insight):** A rethink is sparked when evidence highlights a fundamental gap—such as the user's insight that **"we still don't talk enough about how to govern content generation and publishing."** This is recorded in the **Trigger Log (T-IDs)**. The Trigger is the "Why"; it captures the intent, the human feeling, and the logical necessity for a pivot.
3.  **Trigger to Change (The Commitment):** The actual architectural or procedural shift is recorded in the **Change Log (CH-IDs)**. This defines the "What"—the concrete implementation (e.g., adopting decoupled architecture or "Publishing Protocols").

This pipeline prevents "hidden logic" and ensures that the project's intelligence is navigable. We don't just "change our minds"; we evolve the architecture based on registered evidence and explicitly logged triggers.
