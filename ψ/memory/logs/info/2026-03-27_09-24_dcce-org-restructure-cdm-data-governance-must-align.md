Meeting Report: Internal Structure Reorganization
Department of Climate Change and Environment (DCCE) - 2026

1. Meeting Overview
Subject: Briefing on the Internal Structure Reorganization of the DCCE
Date: Friday, March 20, 2026
Time: 1:30 PM
Location: Room 303-304 (Kannikar - Rajawadee), 3rd Floor, DCCE Building & Zoom
Objective: To explain the new division of labor, updated group missions, and the overall strategic shift for the year 2026.

2. High-Level Organizational Structure
The DCCE is headed by the Director-General, with four specialized groups reporting directly to the top level to ensure oversight and compliance:
- Management System Development Group
- Internal Audit Group
- Legal Group (Adjusted to report directly to the department level)
- Ethics Group (Adjusted to report directly to the department level)

3. Division Missions and Group Tasks

I. Office of the Secretary
Focus: Internal support and administrative backbone.
- Human Resources Management: Workforce planning, recruitment, payroll, and staff development.
- Finance: Accounting, procurement, budgeting, and cost control.
- Planning, Monitoring, and Evaluation: Budget requests, strategy analysis, and tracking project results.
- Executive Support: Managing executive schedules, document filtering, and tracking orders.

II. Strategy and International Cooperation Division
Focus: Policy making and global climate representation.
- Policy and Planning: National CC strategies and status reporting.
- Convention and International Cooperation: Focal Point for UNFCCC and AWGCC; global negotiations.
- Measures and Mechanism Development: Legal and economic tools (ETS, CBAM).
- National CC Policy Committee Secretariat: Secretariat for the "กนก." (National Committee).
- Climate Finance and Investment (New): Managing the National Climate Fund and investment plans.

III. Greenhouse Gas Reduction Drive Division
Focus: Technical mitigation and digital data.
- Policy and Reduction Drive: Setting sector targets and local implementation.
- Monitoring and Evaluation: Tracking reductions and MRV (Measurement, Reporting, and Verification).
- Greenhouse Gas Inventory: Maintaining the official national inventory.
- Reduction Guideline Development: Promoting CCUS and LULUCF guidelines.
- Digital Technology & Central Database: Managing IT infrastructure, cybersecurity, and the Climate Change Information Center (CCIC).

IV. Climate Change Adaptation Drive Division
Focus: Resilience and risk management.
- Policy and Adaptation Drive: Executing the National Adaptation Plan (NAP).
- Risk Information Development: Creating Climate Risk Maps and the Risk Index (CRI).
- Adaptation Monitoring and Evaluation: Operating the Adaptation Platform and gap analysis.
- Adaptation Guideline Development: Promoting local wisdom and ensuring social inclusion.

V. Climate Change and Environment Research Center
Focus: Scientific foundation and capacity building.
- Research Planning and Data Synthesis: Focal Point for the IPCC and expert networking.
- Research and Development: Creating innovations and policy-ready research.
- Laboratory and Modeling: High-resolution climate modeling and environmental testing.
- Personnel Capacity Development: Training programs for both internal staff and external partners.

VI. Participation Promotion Division
Focus: Public engagement and low-carbon education.
- Policy and Participation Drive: Focal Point for ACE (Action for Climate Empowerment).
- Public Participation Promotion: Supporting citizen networks and volunteers (ทสม.).
- Low Carbon City and Economy Promotion: Assisting local authorities and G-Green certification.
- Education and Learning Promotion: AWGEE Focal Point; youth leadership and environmental education.
- Public Awareness: PR campaigns and organizing environmental events.

4. Summary of Major Changes
- Centralization of Oversight: The Legal and Ethics groups were moved to report directly to the department level to increase transparency and authority.
- New Financial Focus: The creation of the Climate Finance and Investment Group highlights a shift toward active fund management and incentivizing green investments.
- Consolidation of Data: Digital and database groups were merged and moved to better support the Greenhouse Gas Reduction division.
- Strengthened Research: The Research Center now has a dedicated group for Personnel Capacity Development to ensure the workforce stays technically skilled.
