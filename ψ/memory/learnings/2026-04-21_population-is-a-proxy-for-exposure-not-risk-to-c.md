# Population is a proxy for Exposure, not Risk. To calculate true risk in dasymetric disaggregation

Population is a proxy for Exposure, not Risk. To calculate true risk in dasymetric disaggregation, population density must be gated by Hazard Probability (using historical frequency or empirical hazard maps). The fundamental correction is the Synthetic Hybrid: Weight = Population * Empirical (Affected-Household). This prevents safe, high-density urban areas from receiving artificial risk scores.
