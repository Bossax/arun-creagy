# Learning: Simple Language in Bureaucratic Workshops

**Date**: 2026-05-05
**Project**: DCCE NCAIF
**Pattern**: Language Simplification for User Feedback

## The Problem
When designing complex systems (like the NCAIF Information Architecture), AI agents and technical consultants tend to use abstract metaphors (e.g., "Monitoring Loop," "Data Enablement Layer," "Knowledge Backbone"). While these terms describe the *logic* of the system, they are alienating to the *users* (government officials and policy makers). This leads to "Expert Drift," where the design moves away from the user's actual needs and language.

## The Solution (The "3-Step Report" Pattern)
To get high-quality feedback in a workshop setting, replace technical system terms with simple service-oriented language:

1.  **Reframe as a Service**: Present the framework as a "Menu of Services" for the user, not a "Data System."
2.  **The 3-Step Question**: Group climate monitoring into three simple buckets:
    *   **Past**: "What happened?" (Damage and Disaster statistics).
    *   **Future**: "What is coming?" (Risk maps and Projections).
    *   **Now/Action**: "What are we doing?" (Progress tracking and Solutions).
3.  **Audit the Use-Case**: Instead of asking if the taxonomy is "correct," ask if it helps the user finish a specific task (e.g., "writing a budget brief") faster.

## Evidence
In the prep for the DCCE workshop (May 2026), moving from "Monitoring Loop" to "3-Step Status Report" immediately clarified the presentation narrative and allowed for more targeted participant questions.
