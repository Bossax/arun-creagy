# CRI Phase 2 v4 Consultant Method Note

## Draft boundary

This file is an implementation-ready outline for the consultant-team method note.

It is written for the team that will maintain the CRI Phase 2 v4 logic, screen candidate indicators, and prepare later communication outputs.

It must stay aligned with the adopted working position in [`CRI_Capacity_Tagging_Dictionary_v4.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRI/output/CRI_Capacity_Tagging_Dictionary_v4.md:21) and the internal guardrail note in [`2026-04-25_interpretation-note-consolidation.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRI/output/asset_indicator_dictionary/experimental_runs/2026-04-25_interpretation-note-consolidation.md:13).

## Core message to hold throughout the note

- Experiment B is the adopted working default.
- Asset indicators and governance indicators are complementary signals of resilience conditions.
- They are not direct formula inputs.
- They are not public linkage-based calculation groups.
- Activation chains remain internal interpretation tools.
- Stronger scores may suggest stronger resilience conditions in general, but they do not prove realized resilience on their own.

## Proposed structure

### 1. Why this note exists
- Explain what changed after the experiment review.
- State who should use this note.
- State what this note is not: not a public communication sheet and not a scoring manual.

### 2. The adopted v4 decision in plain language
- State the choice of Experiment B.
- Explain why the six-pillar kernel stays in place.
- Explain why one narrow overlay survives.
- Explain why the method avoids direct formula language.

### 3. Working model of the index
- Explain asset indicators as condition signals.
- Explain governance indicators as condition signals.
- Explain why the two should be read together.
- State clearly that `R = A × G` is conceptual only if referenced at all.

### 4. What activation chains are for
- Explain that activation chains help the team interpret how stock may or may not become usable function.
- Explain that they support proxy screening, internal review, and narrative examples.
- Explain that they must not be turned into public scoring mechanics.

### 5. Interpretation notes for reuse-first cases
- Summarize the practical reading for HC-01 to HC-04.
- Show how interpretation notes sharpen proxy scanning without creating new concepts.
- Point the reader to the internal consolidation note for the full wording.

### 6. Escalated cases and internal caution
- Summarize why HC-05 to HC-11 needed combination review or overlay review.
- Explain what must remain internal.
- Show how to use these cautions when screening candidate metrics.

### 7. Cross-cutting scored concept rule
- Define `Community Self-Organization & Stewardship` in plain language.
- Explain that it was discovered through HC-09 and HC-11 pressure, but now operates as a cross-cutting scored concept for metric scanning across relevant pillars.
- State that it is not a seventh pillar.
- State that it does not appear as a separate public score.
- State that metrics selected through this concept must be assigned back into existing pillar scores.

### 8. How to screen candidate indicators
- Ask whether the indicator reveals enabling condition, continuity, access, usability, or operational readiness.
- Ask whether the indicator fits the concept definition directly or only through an internal interpretation note.
- Ask whether the indicator captures cross-cutting community self-organization or stewardship signals that would otherwise be missed.
- Ask which existing pillar score the indicator should feed if admitted through the cross-cutting concept.
- Ask whether the indicator risks over-claiming realized resilience.
- Ask whether the indicator is strong enough to stay in public explanation.

### 9. Validation and monitoring caveat
- State that higher scores suggest conditions, not proven outcomes.
- State that activation logic is context dependent.
- State that later monitoring against observed outcomes is required.

### 10. Lineage and boundary record
- Keep Experiment A as the no-growth benchmark.
- Keep Experiment C as the stress-test boundary record.
- Explain what each still teaches the team.
- State clearly that neither replaces the adopted logic.

### 11. Writing controls for later documents
- What can move into DCCE-facing prose.
- What can move into mayor/governor-facing prose.
- What must remain internal.

### 12. Review checklist
- Did the text slip into formula language?
- Did the text treat activation chains as public mechanics?
- Did the text overstate realized resilience?
- Did the text make the cross-cutting concept score-relevant without turning it into a seventh pillar or a visible public score?
