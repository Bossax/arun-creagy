# CRI Phase 2 v4 Shared Scoring Rubric

## 1. Rule of use

Apply this same rubric only after each experiment reaches its final round output.
All dimensions use a 1 to 5 scale.
The default total is **unweighted** with a maximum score of **35**.

## 2. Dimension definitions

| dimension | scoring_question | score_1_low | score_3_mid | score_5_high |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Concept growth | Did the variant avoid unnecessary concept inflation while still solving real linkage gaps? | Many additions with weak necessity or poor pruning | Some additions justified, but inventory remains mixed | Minimal and well-justified delta versus v3 |
| Coverage across pilot assets | Can the variant produce readable activation chains across all 11 hardened concepts? | Many asset chains remain unresolved or unreadable | Most assets are covered, but some chains stay weak | All 11 assets have readable activation chains |
| Bottleneck clarity | Does each chain identify one clear primary bottleneck? | Multiple vague failure points with no clear decision target | Bottlenecks are partly explicit but inconsistent | One primary bottleneck is consistently legible per chain |
| Category purity | Does the variant preserve a clean distinction between asset stock and governance activation? | Frequent drift or category confusion | Some boundary slippage remains but is manageable | Asset and governance categories remain cleanly separated |
| Evidence strength | Are reinterpretations and additions grounded and logged convincingly? | Mostly inferential with thin logging | Mixed evidence quality with partial logging | Strong evidence packages and complete decision logging |
| Cross-asset reusability | Do surviving concepts and linkages work across more than one asset type? | Mostly single-use or highly bespoke | Some reuse across related assets | Broad reuse across diverse assets without distortion |
| Administrative readability | Would a practitioner read the variant as a usable diagnostic rather than a massive audit list? | Too complex, overloaded, or audit-heavy | Usable with caveats and explanation | Concise, decision-oriented, and practitioner-readable |

## 4. Tie-handling note

If totals are close, resolve the decision narratively by examining the joint pattern across evidence strength, category purity, and administrative readability rather than inventing a new weighting scheme midstream.
