## Interpretation

Experiment B concluded that most apparent pressure for concept growth still comes from cross-pillar activation burdens rather than from a truly missing governance family. However, compared with Experiment A, the evidence package was strong enough to preserve one narrower addition for the two softest cases:
- HC-09 food-system and productive bio-resource stock
- HC-11 human and social capability stock

The new concept does **not** operate as a broad sectoral container. It is a tightly bounded overlay for cases where asset activation depends on informal stewardship, customary or local knowledge, trust-based self-organization, and autonomous collective action that are not fully legible inside the six formal readiness categories alone.

This is the main point of divergence from Experiment A. Experiment A treated HC-09 and HC-11 as combination-heavy but still fully foldable into the kernel. Experiment B accepts a small amount of concept growth and concludes that a single community-mediated overlay improves bottleneck clarity without forcing a full redesign of the readiness structure.
