## 3. Canonical framing locked for downstream drafting

- Experiment B is the approved working default.
- The six-pillar institutional-readiness kernel remains the main structure.
- Asset indicators and governance indicators are complementary signals of resilience conditions, not direct formula inputs and not linkage-based calculation groups.
- Activation chains are internal interpretive logic and exemplary narratives only. They are not the public calculation engine.
- Experiment A and Experiment C must be preserved as lineage remarks and boundary evidence, not canonical logic.
- `Community Self-Organization & Stewardship` must be treated as a cross-cutting scored concept in metric scanning and score construction, with selected metrics assigned back into relevant pillar scores.
- Stronger asset and governance scores may suggest stronger resilience conditions in general, but they do not by themselves prove realized resilience. Activation-chain validity is context dependent and requires empirical monitoring against observed resilience outcomes.

## 6. Corrected active integration rule for `Community Self-Organization & Stewardship`

The Friday-night experiment record discovered the pressure point through HC-09 and HC-11. The current active integration rule is broader at the scoring-design level, while still preserving the six-pillar kernel and hiding activation chains from stakeholder-facing outputs.

- It exists because the experiment showed a persistent community-mediated activation burden that the six formal readiness categories did not carry cleanly enough on their own.
- It covers informal stewardship, customary or local knowledge, trust-based self-organization, and autonomous collective action.
- It is not a seventh pillar.
- It is not a separate public score.
- It is not a general community participation bucket.
- It must be used as a cross-cutting scored concept when scanning and admitting metrics across relevant pillars.
- Metrics selected through this concept must be assigned back into existing pillar scores rather than left as narrative only.
- Stakeholder-facing outputs should still show asset scores and six governance pillar scores, not activation chains.
- Its two Round 2 precursor labels remain lineage only: `Community Stewardship & Traditional Knowledge Governance` and `Grassroots Self-Organization & Social Capital` do not survive as separate adopted concepts.

## 7. Lineage and boundary remarks from Experiments A and C

Experiment A and Experiment C stay useful, but only as boundary records.

### What Experiment A preserves

- Experiment A is the no-growth benchmark.
- It shows that all 11 hardened asset concepts can still be carried by the six-pillar kernel with interpretation notes and kernel combinations only.
- It is the main reminder to start with reuse and to resist concept growth unless the gap is persistent and well evidenced.
- It also shows that HC-09 and HC-11 were the softest fits even under the conservative rule set, but not enough to justify concept admission under that rule set.

### What Experiment C preserves

- Experiment C is the stress-test boundary record.
- It shows that looser exploratory admission can temporarily inflate the design space.
- It also shows that very high pruning collapses that inflation back to the same single bounded overlay kept in Experiment B.
- It is the main warning not to treat provisional Round 2 growth as a standing invitation to redesign the taxonomy.

### Shared lineage signal across A, B, and C

- There is no source-backed reason to adopt a broad seventh governance family.
- The durable pressure point is narrow and specific, not taxonomy-wide.
- The adopted logic therefore remains bounded supplementation, not redesign.

## 8. Drafting use for future writers

### What may enter external documents

- Experiment B is the approved working default.
- The six-pillar institutional-readiness kernel remains the main governance structure.
- Asset indicators and governance indicators should be described as complementary signals of resilience conditions.
- External wording may say that stronger asset and governance scores can suggest stronger resilience conditions in general.
- External wording may say that this does not, by itself, prove realized resilience.
- External wording may say that stakeholder-facing outputs show asset scores and six governance pillar scores only.
- External wording may say that community self-organization matters because it changes which governance metrics are selected and scored across relevant pillars, without showing activation chains.

### What must remain internal

- Full activation chains and causal ordering.
- The per-case bottleneck narratives as if they were public scoring mechanics.
- The provisional Round 2 concept labels.
- The detailed pruning logic that merged or removed provisional concepts.
- Any wording that turns the overlay into a seventh pillar, a general community category, or a linkage-based calculation group.
- Any wording that treats higher scores as proof of realized resilience rather than as indicative conditions that still require empirical validation.

### Internal drafting rule of thumb

When writing outward-facing text, keep the message simple:

1. asset stock is potential
2. governance conditions help activate that potential
3. the two together suggest resilience conditions
4. realized resilience still has to be checked against observed outcomes

Anything more detailed than that belongs in internal drafting notes, not in the public-facing calculation story.
