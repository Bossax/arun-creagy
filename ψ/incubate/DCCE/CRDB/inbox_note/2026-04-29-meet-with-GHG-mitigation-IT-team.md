They are formulating a data strategy for DCCE. They plan to engage all divisions within DCCE who hold data assets to stock take what is available and who owns it. They include "all" datasets within DCCE boundary regardless of data owners . The data assets that they pay attention to are machine-readable formats such as csv. Data classification layers are also planned to be established with classification guidelines to aid decision making of the DCCE staff.

They will create a data repository to enhance discovery within DCCE organization. The data owners will be assigned in this project. The data owners are responsible for being the contact person to give detail about the datasets. They are also responsible for updating the datasets. The trigger to update is driven by their need and will not be constitutionalized in this project. 

Data governance in this context means only the governance of data classification and the data hub (the metadata database). They won't touch on QA/QC data standards and formats. The metadata required is generic metadata for data catalog. The categorization of the data catalog will be division based, no data domain tagging. They mentioned that data governance pretty much follows Digital Governance Agency's guideline. %% I don't think they would go into detail of different metadata requirements for different data domains/ entities  %%

They also mention exploring data use cases among DCCE divisions to identify a potential use case to be implemented in this project. This demonstration is to prove that breaking internal slioes can reveal hidden values of data. 

They also aim to update the Climate Change Information Center (CCIC) which aims to serve general public. This platform now hosts data products developed by DCCE and links to other platforms. 


### my opinion
To me, this strategy is similar to creating a enterprise data system based on the data mesh design. I  still cannot see significant amount of value that will be added to DCCE org. Since this project will not establish data lake and data pipelines that define goals and objectives of DCCE in the climate data service ecosystem. This project does not improve data siloes outside the organization, data standards and interoperability. Like garbage in garbage out, if the data owners have low data literacy, they won't be aware of how to make their data assets reusable and high value.
