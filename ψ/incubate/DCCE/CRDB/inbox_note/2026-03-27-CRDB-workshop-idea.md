
"I'm thinking about the workshop in April, which I plan to hold around April 30th. Now, what should the format of the workshop look like? Well, today’s meeting by the TMD (Thai Meteorological Department) on the topic of the National Framework for Climate Services involved bringing stakeholders from across the data value chain together to ask them—since we’ve done our homework and surveyed each organization’s datasets, tools, products, and services—about their climate services, which include hazard, impact, and risk assessment.

The format was essentially calling people to meet, using Mentimeter to conduct polls, and going through their products or services one by one to ask for suggestions or what they need. The facilitation wasn't very complex—just people sitting in rows and going through each person.

So, thinking about our upcoming CRBD workshop, the group of people we’ll invite will be mostly the same people from the same organizations. Although the specific individuals might change, the organizations will be very similar. I'm thinking about how to make the storyline of this workshop continuous and not overlap too much. Actually, I don't want it to overlap; I want it to build upon the previous work.

Right now, we have the TMD workshop discussing the baseline of information products and services. In the next CRBD workshop, how do we build on that? I should mention that for the CRBD workshop, the host is the DCCCE (Department of Climate Change and Environment), which is different from this TMD workshop. For this TMD meeting, the actual host isn't even TMD, but the WMO (World Meteorological Organization), which provided the sponsorship. They are trying to set up the National Framework for Climate Services (NFCS) for Thailand, as it's a product the WMO created and wants countries to adopt.

After today's workshop, we have the CRBD workshop at the end of April, and then another TMD workshop at the end of May to verify or validate the action plan. The output of this project—the National Framework for Climate Services—will be an action plan for setting up the framework in Thailand. This framework essentially defines who does what across the main pillars of the NFCS, such as TMD doing one part and the DCCCE doing another.

The CRBD workshop in April needs to be the bridge between these two, or at least take the baton from today's workshop. Another thing is the facilitation. I want it to have more of a process than today's meeting, which only used Mentimeter polling and didn't have much breakout discussion, which is a shame. We should have more breakout discussions in the CRBD workshop. I need to think more about how to make it not boring and ensure that it's worth the several hours everyone spends together.
