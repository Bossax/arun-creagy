# CRDB Workshop v2 Plan (NFCS Steering Package Oriented)

## 1. Purpose and Audience

This plan turns the approved CRDB–NFCS narrative and WMO/NFCS governance wording into a concrete **CRDB workshop v2** design.

- **Primary audience**: NFCS steering decision-makers and the NFCS coordinating institution (oversight and coordination roles), plus CRDB/DCCE as NCAIF sponsor.
- **Secondary audience**: participating agencies who will implement Human Settlement & Security service bundles and manage baselines, access rails, and UIP elements.

The workshop is explicitly designed as a **validation and convergence gate**, not an initial scoping meeting.

## 2. Non-negotiable Constraints

### 2.1 NFCS Governance and Ownership

- NFCS is **initiated and led by the NMHS** as government-mandated provider of climate services, with **inter-ministerial oversight** providing direction and political support.
- A **coordinating institution** drives development and implementation; CRDB/NCAIF is positioned as an enabling substrate within that governance, not as the host of NFCS.
- Steering-facing artifacts from the workshop must frame outcomes as **recommendations for steering endorsement**, not unilateral decisions by DCCE or TMD.

### 2.2 NCAIF TOR and Website Blueprint

- NCAIF remains **TOR-literal**: the project must deliver a **website blueprint** (sitemap, page templates, content model) recognisable as such to DCCE and procurement.
- The website blueprint is positioned as one element of the **NFCS User Interface Platform (UIP)**: a structured means of engagement and a website containing relevant information, new products, and feedback mechanisms.

## 3. Stakeholder and TOR Assumptions

### 3.1 15–20 Organization Planning Rule

- TOR floor: **at least 15 organizations** represented at the workshop.
- Evidence from interviews and NFCS consultations shows more than 15 candidate organizations across the value chain; **15–20 organizations** is **sensible and achievable**.
- Planning rule:
  - Aim for **15–20 organizations** across functional clusters (below), not 15 as a maximum.
  - If any cluster has **zero confirmed organizations**, treat this as a **red risk** for NFCS-ready claims and adjust the decision scope accordingly.

### 3.2 Functional Stakeholder Clusters

The workshop design embeds the following clusters, consistent with the stakeholder-fit analysis:

1. **Economic and planning**: NESDC, OTP, TBA, NXPO, budget/fiscal actors.
2. **Climate and hazard service providers**: TMD and allied technical units.
3. **Data infrastructure and catalog owners**: DGA, NSO, DCCE/CRDB and related integrators.
4. **Local implementers and design partners**: BMA, DLA and at least one non-Bangkok municipality, UDDC, sector line ministries (e.g. MSDHS, DPT).
5. **Disaster risk and emergency operations**: DDPM and/or provincial DRR actors.
6. **Private sector demand**: FTI and other sector associations.
7. **NFCS oversight and coordination**: NFCS steering/inter-ministerial committee, NFCS coordinating institution.

These clusters are wired into session objectives and breakout composition (Section 6) rather than treated as a separate invite checklist.

## 4. Approved Narrative Logic

### 4.1 Primary Steering Narrative

- **Strategy 2 — Service Bundle Demonstration (impact-based, decision-backwards)** is the primary storyline.
  - Steering sees **1–2 concrete Human Settlement journeys** end-to-end.
  - For each journey the workshop locks minimum service bundles, baselines, and governance needed to make it real.

### 4.2 Packaging of Outputs

Workshop outputs are packaged as:

1. **Strategy 1–style NFCS steering decision package** (≤2 pages)
   - Recommendations for NFCS steering endorsement (not CRDB self-authorization).
2. **Mandatory NCAIF website blueprint annex** (TOR literal)
   - Website is explicitly one UIP element, translating service bundle decisions into a website-facing interface.

### 4.3 Devil s-Advocate Alignment

To reduce steering and sponsor risk, the design explicitly addresses:

- **Decision overload risk**: scope sessions to 1–2 journeys and a small set of decisions per journey (bundles, baselines, roles, rhythm), with an explicit list of deferred decisions.
- **Ownership misperception risk**: presentation script and templates use WMO language (oversight, coordinating institution, NMHS lead) and avoid “CRDB hosts NFCS”.
- **Website tunnel vision risk**: facilitation emphasises that the blueprint is derived from service designs and governance; annex templates force links from pages → bundles → baselines → roles.

## 5. Workshop Structure and Session-by-Session Design

The structure preserves the preliminary five-session spine while making narrative, stakeholder-fit, and devil s-advocate logic explicit.

### Session 1 — Frame and Journeys Confirmation (Plenary)

**Purpose**
- Set NFCS-consistent frame: where this workshop sits between NFCS baseline consultation and subsequent NFCS validation/plan updates.
- Confirm the **1–2 Human Settlement journeys** that will anchor the rest of the day.

**Approx. outputs**
- Agreed description of 1–2 priority journeys (e.g. urban heat risk for operations; flood/compound risk for municipal planning and emergency response).
- Slide(s) that show the **baton-pass timeline** and NCAIF position in the NFCS value chain.

**Stakeholder clusters that must be present**
- Economic/planning: at least NESDC or equivalent.
- NFCS oversight/coordinating: at least one steering or inter-ministerial representative.
- NMHS leadership: TMD representative able to speak to NFCS initiation and climate service leadership.
- Local implementers: at least BMA or another municipality.
- Private sector: at least FTI or sector association.

**Embedded devil s-advocate checks**
- If NFCS oversight or NMHS leadership is absent, the facilitator records a **governance representation risk** and narrows language to “technical recommendations” rather than “steering-ready package”.
- If journeys drift into too many domains, facilitator forces a choice of **1–2 canonical journeys**, with a note that others will use the same pattern post-workshop.

### Session 2 — Demand Backbone and Service Tiers (Breakouts)

**Purpose**
- Validate the decision journeys from the perspective of real workflows, budgets, and incentives.
- Produce **Service Blueprint v0.9** for each journey.

**Core tasks**
- For each journey, small mixed-group tables:
  - map user tiers (strategic, operational, technical);
  - articulate pain points and “what good looks like” using interview language (FTI, UDDC, BMA, DPT, etc.);
  - sketch service steps, actors, and information flows.

**Expected outputs**
- One-page **service blueprint canvas** per journey.
- Ranked **Phase 1 must-have services** for each journey.

**Stakeholder cluster requirements**
- Each breakout must include at least:
  - one local implementer (BMA or non-Bangkok municipality);
  - one planning/engineering actor (e.g. DPT, UDDC, NESDC representation);
  - one private-sector or budget-facing actor (FTI, NESDC, TBA, NXPO);
  - one data/catalog owner (DGA, NSO, DCCE/CRDB).

**Embedded devil s-advocate checks**
- **Tokenisation risk**: facilitation instructions require private sector and local authorities to lead at least one segment of the walkthrough, not just react.
- **Scope creep risk**: if more than two journeys emerge as “priority”, note the extras as **templates to be filled later**; do not expand the workshop decision scope.

### Session 3 — Baselines, Scales, and Access Rails (Breakouts)

**Purpose**
- For each journey, decide **minimum baseline inputs** and **access principles** that are safe for NFCS steering and data holders.

**Core tasks**
- Use a pre-populated list of baseline candidates (from CRDB evidence coverage maps and interim report) and classify each input as:
  - endorsed baseline (NFCS-ready);
  - candidate/experimental dataset;
  - interpreted/derived product with validation needs.
- Capture scale, caveats, and minimum metadata.
- Sketch **access rails** at the principle level (who can see what, at which resolution, under what terms).

**Expected outputs**
- **Baseline classification sheet** per journey.
- Short list of **open questions** that must be resolved before NFCS validation.

**Stakeholder cluster requirements**
- Each breakout must include at least:
  - one data infrastructure or catalog owner (DGA, NSO, DCCE/CRDB);
  - one climate or hazard service provider (TMD or allied technical unit);
  - one sensitive data holder where relevant (e.g. MSDHS, NSO, DDPM);
  - one local implementer to ground scale and usability decisions.

**Embedded devil s-advocate checks**
- **Overreach risk with sensitive data holders**: templates force teams to distinguish between **immediate baseline decisions** and **staged pilots**; access rules for sensitive datasets default to “opt-in pilots” with clear governance notes.
- **Abstraction risk**: at least one column in the baseline sheet ties each baseline to a **concrete decision step** in the Session 2 service blueprint.

### Session 4 — Governance Roles and Operating Rhythm (Breakouts)

**Purpose**
- Turn baseline and access decisions into a **governance role map** and **operating rhythm** aligned with WMO MEL guidance.

**Core tasks**
- For each key data/service component in the journey, assign:
  - oversight body (steering/inter-ministerial); 
  - coordinating institution;
  - owner/steward/custodian/reviewer/approver roles.
- Define minimum **review cadence** and MEL responsibilities, using WMO language on SMART activities and periodic review.

**Expected outputs**
- **Governance role table** per journey.
- **Draft operating rhythm**: bullets on review moments and reporting into NFCS governance forums.

**Stakeholder cluster requirements**
- Breakouts must include:
  - NFCS oversight or coordinating representatives (where possible);
  - NMHS leadership (TMD) and DCCE/CRDB; 
  - data infrastructure owner; 
  - at least one economic/planning actor and one implementer.

**Embedded devil s-advocate checks**
- **Unfunded mandate risk**: each governance line includes a column “resourced owner?”; if “no/unclear”, the recommendation is explicitly qualified and flagged for NFCS steering.
- **Ambiguous NFCS ownership risk**: templates separate **oversight**, **coordinating**, and **implementing** roles, forcing language that mirrors WMO quote bank instead of collapsing everything into “host”.

### Session 5 — Synthesis into Steering Package and Website Annex (Plenary)

**Purpose**
- Convert session outputs into **two clearly scoped deliverables**:
  - NFCS steering decision package (≤2 pages).
  - NCAIF website blueprint annex.

**Core tasks**
- Per journey, short readouts that answer:
  - what we recommend steering endorse (services, baselines, roles, rhythm);
  - what remains open or staged.
- Map outputs into templates from the **Workshop Outputs Pack Skeleton**:
  - fill headline decisions and role summaries directly into the decision package skeleton;
  - confirm which service blueprint and baseline tables become annexes and website pages.

**Expected outputs**
- Draft bullet-level **NFCS steering decision package** content.
- Draft sections and tables for the **website blueprint annex**.

**Embedded devil s-advocate checks**
- **Deliverability risk**: facilitator checks that every element in the steering package draft can be produced by combining workshop artifacts without major re-analysis; otherwise, it is moved to “post-workshop analysis” rather than promised as immediate output.
- **Over-claiming NFCS readiness risk**: if any functional cluster is missing or red risks remain unresolved, the closing script clearly frames outputs as **inputs to NFCS planning** with documented limitations.

## 6. Explicit Outputs per Session and Producer Roles

This section ties sessions to artifacts that drop directly into the outputs pack skeleton.

| Session | Main artifacts | Primary producer roles |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Baton-pass timeline slide, journey selection note | CRDB facilitation team, NFCS oversight/NMHS representatives |
| 2 | Service blueprint v0.9 per journey, Phase 1 must-have list | Mixed stakeholder tables; CRDB team curates |
| 3 | Baseline classification sheets, open questions list | Data owners + climate service providers, with CRDB support |
| 4 | Governance role and rhythm tables | Governance actors (NFCS steering/coordinating, DCCE, TMD, DGA, NESDC, DDPM) |
| 5 | Draft NFCS decision package bullets, website blueprint page list and crosswalk | CRDB secretariat team synthesising from breakout rapporteurs |

These artifacts are structured so that **minimal rewriting** is required to produce the final decision package and annex.

## 7. Post-Workshop Assembly Instructions

After the workshop, the project team will:

1. Use Session 2 service blueprints and Session 3 baseline sheets to populate the **service blueprint one-pagers** and **baseline/access rails table** in the outputs pack skeleton.
2. Use Session 4 governance role tables and MEL bullets to fill the **governance roles + operating rhythm table** and **MEL/accountability block**.
3. Distil Session 5 synthesis into a **2-page NFCS steering decision package**, respecting:
   - NFCS oversight and coordinating language;
   - NMHS leadership framing;
   - explicit staging of sensitive or under-resourced elements.
4. Use the journeys, baselines, and roles to define the **website sitemap, page blueprints, and crosswalk** in the NCAIF website blueprint annex.

## 8. Embedded Devil s-Advocate Risk Register

The following risks and mitigations are not a separate annex; they are **wired into session design and templates**. This list is kept for reference when reviewing the plan.

1. **Too many decision types for available time**
   - Mitigation: limit to 1–2 journeys; pre-define which decisions are in scope; templates include a “deferred decisions” section.
2. **NFCS ownership misperception**
   - Mitigation: steering script and all templates use WMO terms (oversight, coordinating institution, NMHS-led) and avoid “CRDB hosts NFCS”. Oversight and implementation roles are separated in tables.
3. **Website overshadowing governance and baselines**
   - Mitigation: agenda positions website blueprint as an **annex derived from service and governance design**; annex templates require explicit links to bundles, baselines, and MEL.
4. **Stakeholder under-representation (especially private sector, non-Bangkok local authorities, NFCS steering, DRR)**
   - Mitigation: room plan and facilitation notes specify cluster requirements per session; absence of any cluster is logged as a risk and narrows what can be claimed as NFCS-ready.
5. **Sensitive data holder discomfort**
   - Mitigation: baseline/access templates distinguish between immediate endorsement and opt-in pilots; MEL and governance language emphasise safeguards and staged implementation.
6. **Post-workshop drafting overload**
   - Mitigation: all breakout artifacts mirror the **outputs pack skeleton** so that filling the decision package and annex is mechanical.
