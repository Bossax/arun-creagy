# Rationale: NCAIF sitemap v4 — policy-maker journey ordering + “Resources” hub

## What changed (v3 → v4)

1) Re-ordered the **policy-maker entry sequence** so that `National Situation` is the first policy-maker surface (orientation / monitoring loop), followed by `Summary of Risks by Area and Sector` (drill-down summaries).

2) Added a first-class **Resources (Policy / Legal / Funding / Templates)** destination within the policy-maker centre, positioned immediately after the risk summaries.

## Why this change was necessary

- **Progressive disclosure**: policy makers need an initial low-friction, high-salience orientation surface (national monitoring loop) before navigating into detailed risk-by-area / risk-by-sector content.
- **User mental model fit (policy / planning jobs-to-do)**: the ability to quickly find “what rules apply” and “how do I fund / template this” is a core pathway (e.g., “Find Funding”), and it must be visible as a destination rather than buried as incidental mentions.
- **Consistency with existing DCCE navigation precedent**: the as-is DCCE portal includes a policy/reporting hub (“Central Information Center”), indicating that a resource hub is expected and discoverable when treated as a first-class cluster.

## Resulting implication for the policy-maker journey

Policy-maker journey spine becomes:

1. `2.1 National Situation` (monitoring loop)
2. `2.2 Summary of Risks by Area and Sector` (where/what is at risk)
3. `2.3 Resources (Policy / Legal / Funding / Templates)` (how to act within mandates and constraints)
4. `2.4 Data Services for Strategic Planning` (methods, uncertainty, tools)
