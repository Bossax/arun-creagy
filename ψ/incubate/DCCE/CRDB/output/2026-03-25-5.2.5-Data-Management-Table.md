# TOR 5.2.5 – Data Management Structure Table

| Tier | Description | Owner (role) | Update cadence (typical) | Access model (rail) | Key rules / notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Source systems and datasets | Original datasets produced by agencies (hazard models, climate data, socio-economic baselines, disaster records, adaptation project records). | Data owners in line agencies (e.g. technical ministries, NSO, disaster agency, DCCE divisions for their own products). | As defined by each producer (from near-real-time to multi‑year). | Mixed: Internal, G2G (GDX), Open (data.go.th) depending on classification. | Ownership and legal mandate remain with source agencies; CDM and NCAIF reference these datasets but do not override their authority. Classification decisions (Internal/GDX/Open) are made here. |
| 2. CDM-conformant representations | Logical, CDM-aligned representations of key entities and relationships (e.g. `HAZARDOUS_EVENT`, `RISK_ASSESSMENT`, `EXPOSED_ASSET`, `LOSS_DAMAGE_RECORD`, `ADAPTATION_PROJECT`). | DCCE central data and architecture team, with input from domain experts and source agencies. | Updated when new entities or relationships are needed for Phase 1 products; otherwise stable. | Internal (design and documentation) with references exposed via NCAIF methods/metadata pages. | CDM definitions constrain how data are interpreted and combined. Any dataset used in NCAIF must be mapped to CDM entities and subject areas; changes to CDM are versioned, not overwritten. |
| 3. Catalog and metadata layer | Central catalog of datasets and products used by NCAIF, including metadata (owner, source, spatial unit, update cadence, classification, limitations, access path). | Data stewards designated for each subject area; coordinated by DCCE central data team. | Metadata reviewed when datasets change or when new datasets are onboarded; minimum cadence defined per asset (e.g. annual, after major revision). | Mixed: Public metadata visible for open datasets; internal catalog views for G2G and internal assets. | Minimum metadata standard must be met (G2). Each asset has classification (Internal/GDX/Open) and legal/mandate notes where relevant. Catalog entries do not duplicate all data; they point to authoritative locations and describe how to access and use them. |
| 4. Recommended baseline registry | Registry answering “which dataset should I use for this variable/purpose?”, including endorsement, intended use, limitations, version history, and access rail. | Jointly: DCCE central data team + source-agency focal points (endorsers); endorsement authority defined in governance strategy. | Updated when new baselines are endorsed, when methods change, or when prior baselines are superseded; at least when major datasets are updated. | Mirrors underlying dataset’s rail (Open/GDX/Internal), but registry entry itself is at least internally visible. | Implements G3. Each entry records purpose, scale, recommended dataset, known limitations, endorsement roles, and superseded entries. History is preserved (Nothing is Deleted). |
| 5. NCAIF content and profile layer | Structured pages and components such as climate explainers, risk and area profiles, loss-and-damage views, and adaptation planning/implementation sections, each linked to catalog entries and baselines. | Content owners for each NCAIF section (e.g. risk team for Risk & Area Profiles, policy team for Policy Maker Centre); coordinated by NCAIF product owner. | Updated in sync with major baseline changes, new evidence, or scheduled reporting cycles (e.g. annually; after significant events). | Public-facing where appropriate; some profiles or details may be internal depending on underlying data classification. | Each page clearly cites catalog entries and baselines used, includes limitations/uncertainty notes, and respects boundary and crosswalk governance (G4). Changes are tracked so that narrative shifts can be traced back to data changes. |
| 6. User-facing products and packs | Exportable artifacts such as briefing packs, budget justification packs, post-event reports, and thematic explainers, assembled from NCAIF content and registry information. | Product owners (e.g. communication team for public briefs; technical teams for post-event reports) within DCCE. | Produced on demand or according to reporting schedules; updated when underlying data or guidance changes. | Distributed according to content and underlying data classification (e.g. public briefs vs internal reports). | Products must be reproducible from catalog and registry entries. Each pack includes source references, version dates, and limitations. Templates and minimum content rules are defined so that future reports follow the same structure. |

This table summarises the **tiers of the data management structure** from sources to user-facing products, clarifying who owns each tier, how often it changes, how it is accessed, and what governance rules apply.
