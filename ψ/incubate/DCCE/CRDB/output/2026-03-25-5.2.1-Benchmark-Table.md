# TOR 5.2.1 – Benchmark Platform Table

| Platform | Country/Region | Purpose | Key features | Limitations | Implications for NCAIF |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| A-PLAT | Japan | National climate adaptation information platform for government, private sector, and citizens | Multiple entry paths for different user groups; combination of policy information with climate & impact atlases and sector pages; practical resources for local adaptation | Operates primarily as a national portal; does not by itself specify a full national data-governance and CDM structure | NCAIF should adopt user-specific pathways and explanatory content similar to A-PLAT, but place them on top of a conceptual data model and governance framework so that Thai climate-risk information can be integrated and maintained over time. |
| KlimAdapt / KLiVO Portal | Germany | Curated access layer to climate-preparedness services and information products | Service catalogue with filters by planning purpose, field of action, climate variable, impact, region, target group, and category; strong inclusion criteria and metadata discipline | Focuses on routing to external services rather than providing an internal data structure; not directly transferable to Thai institutional landscape | NCAIF should include a catalogue-style layer that tags and routes DCCE and partner products using clear filters and metadata rules, while also maintaining its own internal structure so Thai datasets and services can be governed coherently. |
| Climate-ADAPT | European Union | Multi-level adaptation knowledge hub connecting EU policy, country profiles, tools, indicators, and case studies | Distinct but linked layers for policy pages, data explorers/indicators, tools, and resource catalogues; strong management of sectoral and geographic scale; clearinghouse model linking distributed sources | Designed for EU institutions and data flows; cannot be replicated one-to-one in Thailand; underlying ontology and infrastructure exceed the current CRDB mandate | NCAIF should behave as a national clearinghouse that links distributed datasets, tools, and case libraries across Thai agencies, using metadata and governance rules to ensure that users can find authoritative climate-risk information without centralizing all data into a single system. |

This table is intended as a direct input for Section 1.5 of the interim report. Each row includes a short, explicit sentence on **how the platform informs NCAIF**, satisfying the ready-for-writing criterion for TOR 5.2.1.
