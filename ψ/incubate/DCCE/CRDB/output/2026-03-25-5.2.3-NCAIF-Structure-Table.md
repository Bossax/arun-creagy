# TOR 5.2.3 – NCAIF Minimum Content Categories and Key Entities

| Content category | Description (NCAIF role) | Key CDM subject areas / entities (conceptual) | Typical NCAIF pages / products (from sitemap and design notes) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Climate foundations | Provide curated climate and hazard context so users understand drivers and conditions before looking at risk and adaptation | Subject area 1 – Climate drivers and conditions (climate variables, scenarios, hazard definitions) | Climate science overview pages; hazard explainers; climate trend pages; links to external climate data services (e.g. TMD, GISTDA) with interpretations and limitations. |
| Sector and area risk and adaptation | Show how climate risks and adaptation challenges vary by sector and geography, using both maps and narratives | Subject area 2 – Risk and impact assessment (exposure, vulnerability, risk indices, impact records); linkages to Subject area 4 – Adaptation planning and implementation | Risk and area profile pages (province/area profiles); sector risk pages; provincial comparison views (within constraints); narrative wrappers around the spatial risk map. |
| Loss and damage and post-event information | Connect events, impacts, and losses, providing a bridge between hazard occurrence and socio-economic consequences | Subject area 2 – Risk and impact assessment (event/impact schema, loss records); event-focused extensions guided by data-governance note | Loss and Damage explainer; event overview pages; post-event impact and loss summaries; revision notes showing updates to event records; inputs to post-event reporting packs (MVP-2). |
| Decision-maker briefs and knowledge products | Deliver curated messages, narratives, and cases that help decision-makers use complex evidence without needing to recreate analysis | Primarily Subject area 4 – Adaptation planning and implementation and resilience assessment, with cross-links to other subject areas and Task 5.5 knowledge sets | Executive briefing packs (MVP-1 outputs); budget justification packs; case-study pages; thematic explainers (e.g. cascading impacts, vulnerable groups); use-case-based narratives derived from Task 5.5 List A/B knowledge. |
