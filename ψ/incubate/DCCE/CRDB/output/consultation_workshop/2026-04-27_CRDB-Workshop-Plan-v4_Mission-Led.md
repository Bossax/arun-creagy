# CRDB Workshop Plan v4: The Climate Information Handshake
**Focus**: Connecting Data to Action
**Status**: TOR 5.3.3 Aligned | Mission-Led Design

---

## 1. Design Premise (The "Why")
Previous workshops in this sector often suffer from "Consultation Fatigue"—participants are asked to list datasets in a vacuum, leading to low-value inventories. 

This design pivots to **Co-Production**:
*   **Mission-Led**: We organize people by the "Missions" they are trying to achieve (e.g., City Safety, Economic Security) rather than technical data categories.
*   **Precision-First**: We don't start from zero. We use the "Mirror" (our existing map of 100+ datasets) to force participants to identify specific gaps.
*   **Stress-Tested**: We use a practice run (simulation) to find where the "Data Value Chain" actually breaks in a crisis.
*   **Plain Language**: We remove software and engineering jargon to ensure a National Planner, a Banker, and a Mayor can collaborate on the same process map.

---

## 2. TOR Compliance & Minimum Requirements
This meeting is the formal **TOR 5.3.3 Brainstorming Meeting**.

*   **Attendance**: Must evidence **≥ 80 participants**. 
*   **Evidence Package**: Registration list, sign-in sheets, participant list by organization, group assignment list, photos, and transcribed workshop sheets.
*   **Objective**: Exchange of (1) Datasets, (2) Information Products, and (3) Stated Needs/Demand.
*   **Downstream Links**: 
    *   Outputs feed **TOR 5.3.4** (Product Inventory) and **TOR 5.3.5** (Data Inventory).
    *   Friction points feed **TOR 5.3.8** (Gap Analysis) and **TOR 5.3.9** (Recommendations).

---

## 3. The 4 "Mission Themes" (Table Grouping)
Participants are divided into 8–10 tables. Each table is a mix of makers, experts, and users.

| Theme | Mission | Internal Groundwork |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **A: National Planning** | Protecting the 2030 National Budget & Infrastructure. | Focus: Long-term projections & Authoritative data. |
| **B: Disaster Safety** | Saving lives and assets in the first 48 hours. | Focus: Real-time data & Tactical response. |
| **C: City Life** | Keeping neighborhoods livable (Heat/Flood) at the local level. | Focus: High-resolution maps & Health alerts. |
| **D: Business Security** | Pricing climate risk into loans, insurance, and supply chains. | Focus: Asset-level risk & Financial disclosure. |

## 4. Full Day Schedule

### Morning: The Co-Creation Block (09:00 - 12:00)
*   **09:00–09:15: The Mirror (Plenary)**: Walkthrough of the current data map. Participants add sticky notes to correct/update.
*   **09:15–10:00: The Mission Canvas (Breakouts)**: Tables map their "Ideal Work Process" (Who needs what data, when, and for what decision?).
*   **10:00–10:45: The Practice Run (Breakouts)**: Groups simulate a 2030 flood/heat event to find where their process breaks.
*   **10:45–11:00: The Final Handshake (Plenary)**: Tables present their "Top 1 Fix" for the national system.

### Lunch & Synthesis (12:00 - 13:00)
*   The Project Team assembles the **"Bridge Board"** (The top problems and solutions found in the morning).

### Afternoon: DCCE Briefing & Feedback (13:00 - 15:30)
*   **13:00–13:30: DCCE Briefing**: Presentation on the National Climate Adaptation Information Framework (NCAIF) and data management rules.
*   **13:30–14:30: The Feedback Loop**: Participants use the "Bridge Board" to give feedback on how DCCE's plans can solve the morning's problems.
*   **14:30–15:00: Prioritization**: Voting on the most critical "Unlocks" for the next 12 months.
*   **15:00–15:30: Closing**: Next steps for TOR consolidation and project timeline.

## 5. Facilitation Process & Rules
1.  **The "No Jargon" Rule**: Facilitators must stop any use of technical terms (e.g., NetCDF, RCP8.5) and ask for a plain-language explanation.
2.  **The "So What?" Rule**: If a user asks for data, the facilitator must ask: *"How will this change your final decision?"*
3.  **The "Mirror" Rule**: Don't let participants guess what data exists; point them to the posters and ask them to verify or add to it.
4.  **The "Handshake" Rule**: Ensure Data Makers and Data Users are talking *to* each other, not past each other.
