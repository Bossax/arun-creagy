# Workshop Facilitation Guide: Reviewing the NCAIF Framework (Afternoon Session)
**Target Date**: 2026-05-12
**Goal**: To get feedback from participants on the new NCAIF structure. We need to know if the categories match their needs for planning, budgeting, and reporting.

---

## 1. Presentation Slides: "Tools to Help You Work" (10-15 Minutes)

**Slide 1: NCAIF — The Hub for Climate Data**
*   **Visual**: A simple map showing NCAIF as the center for data from all agencies (TMD, GISTDA, etc.).
*   **Narrative**: "NCAIF is a central hub. It takes scientific data and turns it into information you can use for national policy and reports."

**Slide 2: The National Status Report (3 Steps)**
*   **Visual**: Three icons: 1. Past Damage, 2. Future Risk, 3. Our Progress.
*   **Narrative**: "We have designed a 3-step report to help you track the national situation:
    1. **What happened?** (Past disaster costs).
    2. **What is coming?** (Future risks).
    3. **What are we doing?** (How we are making progress)."

**Slide 3: Getting Resources (Laws and Money)**
*   **Visual**: Icons for the Climate Change Act and Funding Sources (GCF, Thai Environment Fund).
*   **Narrative**: "We provide a library of the **Climate Laws** and a list of **Funding Sources** to help you act on the data and pay for your projects."

**Slide 4: Step-by-Step Planning**
*   **Visual**: A flow chart: Science → Risks → Damage → Planning → Tracking.
*   **Narrative**: "This section follows the way you work. It starts with the **Science**, helps you assess the **Impact**, and ends with choosing the right **Solutions** (like planting trees or building walls)."

**Slide 5: Special Tools (The 4 "Engines")**
*   **Visual**: 4 simple icons for the specialized tools DCCE is building.
*   **Narrative**: "DCCE is building 4 special tools to power this website—including a data catalog and a tool to help you interpret risk maps."

**Slide 6: Instructions for Feedback**
*   **Visual**: A picture of the poster and sticky notes.
*   **Narrative**: "Now, we will ask for your opinion using your phones (Mentimeter) and by putting sticky notes on the posters around the room."

---

## 2. Mentimeter Questions: Simple Opinion Polls

### Question 1: What is most useful for reports?
*   **Question**: "Which part of our 3-step report (Past, Future, Action) is most useful for the reports you give your boss?"
*   **Options**:
    1. Past Damage (Disaster statistics).
    2. Future Risk (Risk maps).
    3. Our Progress (Tracking what we have done).

### Question 2: What information do you need for a Province?
*   **Question**: "When looking at a Province, what do you need most?"
*   **Scale**: (Left) **Technical Facts** (Why it is risky) <---> (Right) **Economic Impact** (How much it will cost us).

### Question 3: Is anything missing?
*   **Question**: "Is there a specific sector or dataset you need for your work that is **missing** from our map?"
*   **Format**: Word Cloud (Participants type simple words like 'Energy' or 'Transport').

---

## 3. Poster Activities: Group Feedback
*Place 3 large posters of the Sitemap around the room.*

### Activity A: "Where is your work?"
*   **Instruction**: "Take a sticky note. Write one **Report** or **Decision** you have to make every year. Stick it on the part of the map that would help you do that job faster."
*   **Goal**: To see which sections are most useful and which are not needed.

### Activity B: "Check the Steps"
*   **Instruction**: "Look at the 'Planning' and 'Action' sections. Do these steps match how your agency actually works? If a step is missing, write it on a sticky note."
*   **Goal**: To make sure the planning steps are realistic.

### Activity C: "Is the language clear?"
*   **Instruction**: "Use a **Red Sticker** on any word or category that is confusing or uses too much 'Consultant Jargon'."
*   **Goal**: To make sure the website is easy for everyone to understand.
