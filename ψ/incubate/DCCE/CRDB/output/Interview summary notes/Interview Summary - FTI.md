### 1. Role and Responsibility of the Agency

- **Key duty and roles:** The FTI represents the private industrial sector. For climate initiatives, they operate via two main institutes. The **Climate Change Institute** focuses on four pillars: platform development (CFO/CFP), academy and consulting (training), research (ETS, CBAM), and climate action (decarbonization roadmaps). The **Water and Environment Institute** focuses on sustainable water management, specifically ensuring long-term water security for industrial investments against climate change factors.
    
- **Associated legal frameworks:** They are closely monitoring the upcoming Climate Change Act. Currently, since there is no enforced mandate, their carbon trading platform (FTX) is paused, and they focus on voluntary greenhouse gas assessments and capacity building.
    

### 2. Highlighted Key Projects

- **Green Finance for SMEs (with Thai Bankers' Association):**
    
    - _Status & Scope:_ Active. A 5,000 million THB credit line in collaboration with 9 banks to provide climate finance for SMEs.
        
    - _Data Integration:_ FTI assesses the SMEs' carbon emissions (Scope 1 and 2) before and after the loan to prove emission reductions, which the banks use to justify the green loan rates.
        
- **Decarbonization Roadmap:**
    
    - _Status & Scope:_ Active. Developing sector-specific decarbonization roadmaps in collaboration with the Thailand Greenhouse Gas Management Organization (TGO).
        
- **Water Risk & Management War Room (EEC Focus):**
    
    - _Status & Scope:_ Active. Collaborating with 9 national water agencies (e.g., Royal Irrigation Department, ONWR) and major industrial players in the Eastern Economic Corridor (EEC).
        
    - _Scope:_ They integrate national water supply forecasts with 10+ years of industrial water demand data to evaluate 20-year water scarcity risks.
        

### 3. Current Workflow & Data Usage

- **Context and Hazard Scope:** FTI historically focuses heavily on water risks (drought and flooding) but is rapidly shifting to address cascading risks like heatwaves (impacting labor/productivity) and sea-level rise (specifically Bangkok sinking).
    
- **Data Sources & Modeling Techniques:** The Water Institute models water demand versus supply by pulling baseline data from government agencies and matching it with their internal industrial water demand data. They forecast short-term (1-5 years) and long-term (20 years) scenarios to determine if tech interventions are needed to prevent water shortages.
    
- **Inter-agency Collaborations:** They operate as a bridge, synthesizing raw scientific data from ONWR and the Hydro Informatics Institute (HII / สสน.) into actionable business intelligence for industrial estates.
    

### 4. Climate Data Needs & Gaps

- **The "Clearinghouse" & Verification Gap:** FTI stressed that water data comes from too many uncoordinated agencies. They urgently need DCCE or a central body to verify and declare a "single source of truth" for hazard data so the private sector knows which dataset to trust for financial planning.
    
- **Severe Communication & UX Roadblocks:** FTI heavily criticized the current state of government digital infrastructure. They noted that DCCE's website is frequently broken, contact numbers go unanswered, and high staff turnover causes massive knowledge loss. They strongly requested an interactive portal with chatbots and up-to-date contacts, referencing EU platforms as the standard.
    
- **Incentives for Private Sector Data Sharing:** DCCE cannot rely solely on the upcoming Climate Change Act to force the private sector to share data. FTI emphasized that DCCE must clearly communicate the _business value_ of data sharing to the private sector and suggested building a "Data User Community" to share analytics and foster collaboration.
    
- **Specific Requests for DCCE:** * **Thailand Risk Map:** Provide a granular, 10 sq km location-based AND sector-based risk map.
    
    - **Impact Assessment Guidelines:** Develop clear manuals teaching businesses and local governments _how_ to calculate the financial impact of a climate hazard (e.g., how to translate "days flooded" into "business interruption costs").
        

### 5. Next Steps & CRDB Integration

- **Map to Implementation Plan:** FTI's severe frustration with broken government websites and unverified data perfectly validates the core mission of the WP4 Data Catalog (providing a single, reliable source of truth) and WP3 Data Governance (establishing clear data stewards to prevent knowledge loss during staff turnover).
    
- **Seed Use Cases for WP3 Conceptual Data Model (CDM):** * _Scenario 1:_ "Projecting 20-year water scarcity risks in the EEC by overlaying industrial water demand against climate-adjusted supply forecasts to trigger tech investments."
    
    - _Scenario 2:_ "Translating a 10 sq km heatwave projection into direct business interruption costs and labor productivity loss for manufacturing facilities."
        
- **Planned Participation:** FTI confirmed their willingness to participate in the upcoming post-Songkran April workshop to help validate the proposed data products and structure.
