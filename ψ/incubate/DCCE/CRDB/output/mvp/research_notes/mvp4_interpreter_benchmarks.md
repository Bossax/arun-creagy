# MVP-4 (Interpreter) Benchmarking: Real-World Relatives

## 1. Identified Relatives
*   **IPCC Uncertainty Lexicon**: Standardized terms (Very Likely, High Confidence) to quantify spread for non-experts.
*   **NOAA Toolkit**: Color-coded "Fitness for Purpose" tiers and icons for risk levels.
*   **Copernicus/CCSP**: "Suitability for Use" labels that align data quality with administrative action.

## 2. Design Patterns (Level 1 vs Level 2)
| Feature | Level 1: Decision Readiness | Level 2: Statistical Shield |
|---------|-----------------------------|----------------------------|
| **Labeling** | "Ready for Regional Planning" (Tactical) | "Ensemble Spread: High" (Technical) |
| **Visuals** | Color-coded Traffic Lights (Green/Amber) | Fan Charts, Probabilistic Curves |
| **Guidance** | "Suitable for Scoping, Not Design" | Confidence Interval Explainer |

## 3. Confidence Score: 5/5
**Rationale**: Very strong evidence for the "Readiness Label" pattern in IPCC/NOAA. The idea of translating statistical spreads into qualitative "Confidence" terms for administrators is a gold-standard benchmark.
