# TOR 5.2.3 – Draft NCAIF Structure (Working Narrative)

This note consolidates the internal narrative for the NCAIF structure, aligned to TOR 5.2.3 and v1 main report: Ch.1 §§1.8–1.9, using the design record in [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/National Climate Adaptation Information Framework.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/National%20Climate%20Adaptation%20Information%20Framework.md), the Pack A/B/C decisions in [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-12 - NCAIF_Pack_ABC_Decision_Matrix.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/2026-03-12%20-%20NCAIF_Pack_ABC_Decision_Matrix.md), and the use-case synthesis in [`ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/NCAIF_Use_Cases.md`](ψ/incubate/DCCE/CRDB/output/NCAIF_Use_Cases.md).

It is written as English working prose that can be adapted into Thai when drafting Section 1.

## 1. NCAIF as an adaptation information framework, not just a website

The National Climate Adaptation Information Framework (NCAIF) is defined in the v1 report as the organizational backbone for climate-risk and adaptation information in Thailand. It is not only a new website or portal. Instead, it combines:

- a business taxonomy that describes how climate, risk, loss and damage, adaptation actions, and results relate to one another; and
- a set of workflow-oriented entry points that make these relationships usable for different stakeholders.

The v1 report (Ch.1 §§1.8–1.9) positions NCAIF as the visible expression of the conceptual data model (CDM) and the data-governance strategy. CDM provides the subject areas and entities; NCAIF translates them into pages, navigation, and products that non-technical users can work with.

FGD1 and FGD2 confirm this interpretation:

- FGD1 shows that DCCE already has many systems and products, but they are fragmented and do not form a coherent adaptation information service.
- FGD2 makes clear that leadership expects the project to deliver a concrete platform structure (sitemap, profiles, briefing packs), while also recognising that Phase 1 is primarily about logical and governance structure rather than a fully built system.

NCAIF therefore sits at the intersection of three elements:

1. existing DCCE and partner assets (risk maps, reports, data systems);
2. a conceptual backbone (CDM subject areas and entities); and
3. workflow-driven products (briefing packs, recommended baselines, loss-and-damage views) that respond to the use cases in the NCAIF use-case inventory.

## 2. Layers and subject areas in the draft NCAIF structure

### 2.1 CDM subject areas and their reflection in NCAIF

The CDM, as summarised in v1 Ch.1 §1.8 and related notes, divides the adaptation information domain into four main subject areas:

1. Climate drivers and conditions – observations and projections of climate variables and hazards.
2. Risk and impact assessment – exposure, vulnerability, risk outputs, and impact/loss records.
3. Resilience assessment – indicators and assessments of adaptive capacity and resilience.
4. Adaptation planning and implementation – plans, projects, measures, and monitoring of results.

NCAIF does not expose these subject areas as raw database tables. Instead, the sitemap and content structure translate them into user-facing sections:

- Climate science and hazard explainers correspond to the climate drivers and conditions subject area.
- Risk and area profiles, including the spatial risk map and narrative profiles, correspond to the risk and impact assessment subject area.
- Loss and Damage explainers and post-event pages connect risk outputs to event-based impact narratives.
- Adaptation planning and implementation sections reflect the planning and implementation subject area.
- Policy maker centre pages and indicators cut across subject areas to provide decision-ready summaries.

This mapping ensures that every user-facing page type can be traced back to a specific part of the CDM, even when the underlying data may live in external systems.

### 2.2 Hybrid sitemap: backbone and entry points

The NCAIF document and the Pack A/B/C decision matrix converge on a hybrid architecture:

- The adaptation cycle (climate → risk → loss and damage → planning → implementation → results) is the main backbone.
- A Policy Maker Centre provides a decision-ready entry point with national and provincial summaries, indicators, and recommended baselines.
- Risk and Area Profiles provide geography-first entry, centred on provincial and area-level profiles linked to the risk map and contextual information.
- An Adaptation Measures and Implementation strand surfaces who is doing what, using existing case materials and programme information.
- A Knowledge, tools, and data services layer groups methods, standards, catalogues, and tools that support analysts and implementers.

Pack A (product reality), Pack B (TOR framing), and Pack C (UI/UX evidence) jointly support this hybrid approach: the structure keeps the adaptation cycle and policy-maker block from earlier designs, but refines navigation so that tools and data are not mixed, and advanced analytic features are treated as secondary.

## 3. Minimum content categories in NCAIF

Across the NCAIF design note, the Pack A/B/C decisions, and the use-case inventory, four minimum content categories emerge as the foundation for Phase 1:

1. Climate foundations – climate drivers, scenarios, hazard overviews, and key climate indicators.
2. Sector and area risk and adaptation – risk and area profiles, sectoral risk narratives, and adaptation context.
3. Loss and damage and post-event information – event-focused summaries, impact narratives, and loss/damage views.
4. Decision-maker briefs and knowledge products – briefing packs, case studies, and explanatory content derived from Task 5.5 knowledge sets.

These categories correspond to the Phase 1 expectation that NCAIF should:

- help users understand what the climate and risk situation is;
- show how risks differ by sector and area;
- explain what has happened and what it cost when events occur; and
- provide usable narrative and analytic products that support decisions and communication.

The CDM and data-governance strategy ensure that each category has clear underlying entities, ownership, and quality rules, even when the front-end expression is a narrative page or downloadable pack.

## 4. Relationship to workflow patterns and MVPs

The workflow patterns and MVP mapping in the NCAIF document link the structural layers above to concrete service expressions. Four cross-cutting workflow patterns (P1–P4) are prioritised:

- P1 – Curated briefing pack for policy, decision, and budget → MVP-1 Briefing Pack Generator.
- P2 – Event → impact → loss/damage post-event assessment → MVP-2 Post-event reporting pack.
- P3 – Finding and agreeing on the official dataset → MVP-3 Recommended Dataset Registry.
- P4 – Uncertainty-safe risk analysis for advanced users → MVP-4 Uncertainty guidance and publishing standard.

These MVPs sit within the NCAIF structure as follows:

- The Policy Maker Centre and Adaptation information by cycle house the briefing packs and budget justification packs (MVP-1), especially in risk, planning, and implementation subsections.
- The Loss and Damage segment under the adaptation cycle houses the post-event reporting and revision views (MVP-2), linking events to baselines and methods.
- The Knowledge, tools, and data services layer houses the Recommended Baseline Registry (MVP-3), which acts as the clearinghouse for endorsed datasets.
- The same layer, and relevant hazard/risk pages, expose uncertainty guidance and publishing standards (MVP-4), ensuring that probabilistic layers and indices are interpreted correctly.

In other words, the NCAIF structure is not just a list of pages; it embeds these MVPs so that key workflows have a visible “home” in the sitemap, while the CDM and governance notes describe how the underlying data are organised.

## 5. How the sitemap (Appendix ข) expresses the structure

Appendix ข of the v1 report records the draft NCAIF sitemap. The NCAIF design note summarises its current form as a top-level navigation with items such as:

- Home
- Data Management Centre for Policy Makers
- Adaptation Information by Cycle
- Risk and Area Profiles
- Adaptation Measures and Implementation
- Knowledge, Tools, and Data Services
- News, updates, and about pages

Within this sitemap:

- The Home page introduces national priorities and offers quick entry by user need.
- The Data Management Centre for Policy Makers concentrates national summaries, indicators (including disaster and adaptation indicators), status of measures, and strategic data services.
- The Adaptation cycle section arranges climate, risk, loss and damage, planning, and implementation pages in a logical sequence that mirrors the conceptual subject areas.
- The Risk and Area Profiles section wraps the spatial risk map product in narrative profile pages, with explicit caveats about scale and interpretation.
- The Adaptation Measures and Implementation and Knowledge/Tools/Data Services sections act as the main presentation layers for Task 5.5 knowledge outputs and data-service catalogues.

Pack A/B/C decisions refine this sitemap by:

- emphasising province profiles and briefing packs as essential content wrappers;
- separating tool discovery from data catalog and download; and
- making limitations, uncertainty guidance, and standards visible as support pages rather than hidden in PDFs.

The interim report does not need to reproduce the full sitemap diagram, but Section 1.9 should describe this structure in words. This narrative provides the underlying logic so that the Thai report text can explain what main sections exist, which workflows they support, and how they tie back to the CDM and governance structure.

## 6. Linkage to data governance and management structure

The NCAIF structure is explicitly linked to the data-management architecture described in v1 Ch.1 §1.10 and the governance strategy note. Key linkages include:

- Recommended baseline registry (MVP-3) – anchors the “clearinghouse vs repository” stance and defines how endorsed datasets are surfaced in NCAIF sections.
- Boundary and crosswalk governance – ensures that risk and area profiles and event/loss views use consistent spatial references.
- Publishing rails (open, GDX, internal) – constrain where datasets can appear in NCAIF and how they are described.
- Metadata and QA/QC rules – underpin the catalog and data-service strands in the Knowledge/Tools layer.

For drafting Section 1, this means the NCAIF structure must always be described together with its management and governance implications, not as a purely visual sitemap. The framework is, by design, both an information architecture and a management structure.
