## 1) Executive summary (what the gap analysis reveals)

The current DCCE website is **administration‑first and portal‑of‑portals**. It contains extensive policy documents, national reports, and a large ecosystem of specialized technical systems (CCIC, Risk MAP, SCP portals), but these assets are **fragmented and not organized into an adaptation‑cycle narrative** or a decision‑maker “front door.”

The NCAIF vNext requires:

- a **policy‑maker landing area** that surfaces decision‑ready summaries
- a visible **adaptation‑cycle backbone** (climate → risk → loss/damage → planning → implementation → results)
- **area profiles** and **briefing packs** as the primary user‑facing products
- **embedded methods and data services**, not siloed portals

These elements are either **missing** or **present only as documents** in the current site.

## 2) Key structural gaps (by NCAIF section)

1) **Policy Maker Center** — weak/under‑specified
   - No consolidated national summary, indicators dashboard, or “status of measures” view.
   - Content exists only as PDFs or dispersed reports.

2) **Adaptation Information by Cycle** — fragmented
   - Climate, risk, and adaptation content exist, but not as a **coherent cycle**.
   - Loss and damage is not a dedicated user‑facing node.

3) **Risk and Area Profiles** — partial
   - Risk MAP exists, but no narrative profile pages, comparison views, or export‑ready packs.

4) **Adaptation Measures & Implementation** — missing or scattered
   - Case studies exist in news but no structured options library or “who is doing what” registry.

5) **Knowledge/Tools/Data Services** — fragmented
   - Many tools exist (CCIC, data catalog, SCP portals), but discovery is fragmented by subdomain and program silo.

## 3) Journey‑level impacts

### Policy maker

Blocked or weak:
- No unified “executive summary” for risk and adaptation status.
- No simple path to **briefing packs** or **policy‑oriented indicators**.

### Technical analyst

Blocked or weak:
- Data tools exist but are **distributed and inconsistent**.
- Methods and standards are embedded in PDFs rather than explicit guidance pages.

### Local implementer

Blocked or weak:
- No “area profile” landing page or local adaptation evidence packs.
- Case studies and options are scattered across program pages and news.

## 4) Phase‑1 priorities (bridging actions)

### Priority A — Quick IA fixes (no new data required)

1. Add a **Policy Maker Center** landing block on the homepage.
2. Add a **single Adaptation Cycle overview page** linking existing content.
3. Re‑label and cross‑link Risk MAP and CCIC under “Risk and Area Profiles.”

### Priority B — Minimal new content pages

1. Create **Loss and Damage** explainer page (post‑event assessment + update logic).
2. Create **Briefing Pack** template page (downloadable summary with limitations).
3. Create **Adaptation Measures Library** page with curated case links.

### Priority C — Strategic rewiring (Phase‑1 architecture)

1. Consolidate discovery of CCIC, Risk MAP, and Data Catalog into a **single entry hub**.
2. Introduce **metadata‑first navigation** (by area, sector, hazard) rather than only by program.
