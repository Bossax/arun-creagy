## Executive summary

The edited draft has a strong high-level structure and a usable strategic arc, but chapters 1 to 3 still have four core weaknesses:

1. Audience-facing prose repeatedly slips into writer-facing logic
2. Several causal claims are asserted without enough bridge logic or evidence framing
3. The scenario architecture in chapter 3 is not yet stable enough for a polished foresight report
4. The draft currently has no usable citation apparatus, which means many claims cannot yet be defended as a formal report

This is not mainly a copyediting problem. It is a substantive revision problem involving voice, logic, structure, and foresight design.

## Bottom-line diagnosis

The main problem is not lack of structure. The main problem is instability of voice and logic. The edited draft still oscillates between three modes:

1. analytical report language
2. drafting-process language
3. abstract AI-like language

The next pass should therefore be a substantive rewrite pass rather than a light copyedit pass.

## Additional search questions needed before rewriting

These are the priority research questions that would strengthen chapters 1 to 3 before the next drafting pass.

### 1. Vision and target-outcome questions

- What exact future condition should the report use to define a good life trajectory for children and youth in the southern border provinces by 2590
- What specific outcomes should be emphasized for the primary target groups, especially orphans and other vulnerable youth
- Which outcomes matter most for this report: learning continuity, safety, belonging, mental wellbeing, employability, income security, civic trust, or some combination
- How should the report distinguish between generic youth development and the specific future of children and youth in this regional context

### 2. Vulnerability and conflict-context questions

- How does prolonged violence or insecurity shape childhood vulnerability in the three southern border provinces
- How do identity, language, religion, and political history affect children’s access to education, welfare, and protection
- What pathways turn regional conflict conditions into accumulated risk for children and youth
- How do these mechanisms differ for orphans, children in fragile households, and youth outside formal learning pathways
- What evidence exists on how fear, surveillance, mistrust, or social fragmentation affect willingness to engage with public systems

### 3. Access, trust, and service-use questions

- What concrete mechanisms cause service access barriers to reduce trust in public institutions
- What kinds of access barriers matter most in this context: documents, travel, language, safety, stigma, cost, bureaucratic complexity, or data-sharing fears
- Are there documented cases showing that children or caregivers avoid public services even when they are formally entitled to them
- What kinds of institutions or intermediaries are actually trusted by families and youth in the area
- Under what conditions does trust improve rather than deteriorate

### 4. Learning-pathway and competency-recognition questions

- What are the real learning pathways available to children and youth in the three provinces
- Where do transitions break down between state schools, religious schools, informal learning, community learning, and work pathways
- What forms of competency recognition or transferability already exist, if any
- What barriers prevent alternative learning pathways from converting into real educational or livelihood opportunities
- What evidence shows how children move between pathways, or fail to move between them

### 5. Economy and livelihood questions

- What are the main livelihood structures shaping household decisions about children’s education and work
- What forms of work or income opportunity are realistically available to youth in and outside the region
- Which emerging sectors are actually relevant: halal economy, creative economy, digital work, cross-border trade, care work, local services, or others
- How accessible are these opportunities to vulnerable youth rather than only well-positioned youth
- What evidence exists on youth out-migration, who leaves, why they leave, and what this means for the region

### 6. Belonging, culture, and identity questions

- How does Malay Muslim identity function in practice as a source of belonging, constraint, social protection, or economic value
- Should cultural belonging be analyzed separately from cultural capital in the scenario framework
- How do language, cultural legitimacy, and social recognition affect whether youth remain in learning systems or withdraw from them
- What forms of identity-based inclusion or exclusion are visible in the evidence base

### 7. Digital opportunity and digital-risk questions

- How are children and youth in the area actually using digital tools for learning, work, social connection, and survival
- What are the main digital risks facing vulnerable youth in this setting
- Which households are most exposed to digital exclusion and why
- What role do caregivers, teachers, community institutions, or peer networks play in digital protection
- What would justify treating digital as both opportunity infrastructure and a child-protection environment in this report

### 8. Weak-signal and future-driver questions

- What weak signals are genuinely emerging in the region rather than imported from generic futures discourse
- Which signals show early shifts in youth identity, learning behavior, mobility, digital life, or trust in institutions
- Which megatrends most strongly interact with local drivers: demography, climate, AI, labor-market change, state capacity, religio-cultural change, migration, or regional integration
- How should the report connect megatrends with the system-map nodes rather than discussing them separately

### 9. Fairness, protection, and orphan-support questions

- What controversies or grey areas exist around the definition, recognition, or support eligibility of orphans and vulnerable children
- Are there mismatches between formal categories and lived realities that create exclusion or perceived unfairness
- What forms of assistance are available through state, religious, community, or civil-society channels
- Where do children fall through the cracks between these systems
- What evidence exists on how families perceive fairness, bias, or inconsistency in child support systems

### 10. Scenario-design questions

- What are the best two uncertainty axes for building scenarios that are both plausible and decision-useful
- Is the current economic axis too binary, and if so what alternative axis would better reflect real futures
- Should the second axis be about distribution of opportunity, territorial embeddedness, quality of institutions, or degree of trust rather than a simple traditional-versus-high-value economy split
- What scenario names would be memorable without becoming gimmicky or weakening report seriousness
- Which scenario indicators are essential for comparing futures of children and youth in this region

### 11. Desirable-future framing questions

- What exact phrases can define a desirable future in concrete and policy-relevant Thai
- What does real access mean in this report for education, welfare, protection, and work pathways
- What does an inclusive halal, creative, or digital economy mean in operational terms for vulnerable youth
- What does it mean for learning from different pathways to carry equivalent weight in practice
- What institutional conditions would make the desirable future believable rather than aspirational only

### 12. Evidence-traceability questions

- Which claims in chapters 1 to 3 already have source backing and which are still synthesis claims needing evidence support
- Which quantitative claims require verification before inclusion in the next draft
- Which qualitative assertions should be anchored to interviews, workshop findings, transcripts, or prior project notes
- What sources should be treated as ground truth for the next rewrite pass
- Which paragraphs can already support author-year inline citations and which cannot
- Which claims require primary project materials rather than web-discovered references
- Which references are foundational enough to appear repeatedly across chapters 1 to 3

### A. Best done via Perplexity

Use Perplexity when the task is primarily about fast discovery, fact-finding, finding source leads, checking terminology, locating reports, or getting a quick evidence scan.

#### 1. Fast factual grounding
- What official or research sources exist on children and youth in the three southern border provinces
- What published evidence exists on school participation, poverty, orphanhood, migration, youth employment, or digital access in the region
- What official definitions are used for vulnerable children, orphans, child protection, competency recognition, or alternative education
- What policy documents define the halal economy, creative economy, digital economy, or youth-skills policy in Thailand

#### 2. Source discovery for later synthesis
- Which reports discuss access barriers to welfare, education, and protection in the southern border provinces
- Which studies discuss trust in public institutions, conflict impacts, or community-state relations in the region
- Which research covers religious schools, informal learning, or pathway fragmentation in southern Thailand
- Which sources discuss youth out-migration, local labor-market structure, and cross-border opportunity patterns

#### 3. Quick comparator questions
- What international or regional examples exist for competency recognition across diverse learning pathways
- What examples exist of child-protection systems working in fragile or conflict-affected settings
- What examples exist of digital safety frameworks for vulnerable youth
- What examples exist of culture-linked local economic development that includes marginalized youth

#### 4. Terminology and concept clarification
- What is the most precise policy meaning of terms such as competency recognition, alternative learning pathways, digital inclusion, child-protection environment, or social trust
- How do major organizations define belonging, resilience, wellbeing, or youth transition in fragile settings

### B. Best done by Gemini Deep Research

Use Gemini Deep Research when the question needs multi-source synthesis, contradiction handling, concept integration, structured comparison, or a tailored analytical output rather than quick fact retrieval.

#### 1. Conflict-vulnerability-access-trust synthesis
- How prolonged conflict, insecurity, identity, language, religion, and political history combine to shape children’s vulnerability in the three southern border provinces
- How access barriers translate into distrust, withdrawal, or selective use of institutions
- How these mechanisms differ for orphans, fragile households, and youth outside formal learning systems

#### 2. Learning-system synthesis
- How state schools, religious schools, informal learning, community learning, and work pathways interact in practice
- Where transition bottlenecks occur and how they affect long-term youth opportunities
- What a realistic framework for competency recognition would need to include in this regional context

#### 3. Economy-opportunity synthesis
- Whether the current economic scenario axis is too binary and what more realistic alternatives exist
- How youth livelihood futures should be framed in a way that reflects actual opportunity distribution, not just sector growth
- How halal, creative, digital, and cross-border opportunities differ in accessibility for vulnerable youth

#### 4. Belonging-identity-cultural capital synthesis
- How Malay Muslim identity functions differently as belonging, social protection, cultural legitimacy, economic resource, or exclusion mechanism
- Whether cultural belonging should be separated from cultural capital in the scenario framework

#### 5. Weak signals and megatrends synthesis
- Which megatrends matter most for this region and how they interact with local drivers
- Which weak signals are genuinely emerging from the local context rather than imported from generic futures discourse
- How megatrends should be connected to the system-map nodes in a coherent foresight frame

#### 6. Scenario-design synthesis
- What two uncertainty axes would best generate plausible and decision-useful scenarios for this report

### Brief 1 — Conflict–vulnerability–access–trust synthesis
