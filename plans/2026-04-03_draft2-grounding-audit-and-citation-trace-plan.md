## Grounding test standard

Every substantive Draft 2 paragraph must pass these checks:

1. `Claim exists`
   - paragraph has a `CLAIM_ID` in [`ψ/lab/foresight-report-wrting/citations/report-claim-evidence-map.md`](ψ/lab/foresight-report-wrting/citations/archive/report-claim-evidence-map.md)
2. `Artifact evidence exists`
   - `CLAIM_ID` points to one or more `ART_PAR_ID`s in [`ψ/lab/foresight-report-wrting/citations/artifact-paragraph-source-map.md`](ψ/lab/foresight-report-wrting/citations/archive/artifact-paragraph-source-map.md)
3. `Canonical source exists`
   - each `ART_PAR_ID` resolves to one or more real `SRC_ID`s in [`ψ/lab/foresight-report-wrting/citations/citation-registry.md`](ψ/lab/foresight-report-wrting/citations/archive/citation-registry.md)
4. `Wording does not overclaim`
   - prose in the Draft 2 paragraph is no broader than the evidence behind it
5. `Status is explicit`
   - paragraph is either `citation-ready`, `weak synthesis`, or `HOLD`

## Risk controls

- Do not assume a paragraph is grounded just because it sounds plausible.
- Do not assume a research note is grounded just because it has a `Works cited` section.
- Do not allow a claim to inherit all sources cited in a note unless the paragraph-level phrase actually depends on them.
- Do not use internal memos as the final ground truth when underlying external sources can be resolved.
- Do not add inline citations to the integrated report unless the full chain is complete.

## Acceptance criteria

This plan is complete only when:

- every Draft 2 file has an audit status,
- every high-stakes paragraph has a `CLAIM_ID`,
- every such claim has at least one `ART_PAR_ID`,
- every `ART_PAR_ID` resolves to real `SRC_ID`s or is explicitly marked weak or HOLD,
- and the integrated report can distinguish grounded text from synthesis scaffolding.
