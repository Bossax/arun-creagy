# CRDB Workshop — TOR 5.3.3 Compliance Checklist (execution-ready)

**Purpose**: A practical checklist to ensure the workshop can be evidenced as the **TOR 5.3.3 brainstorming meeting** and that outputs can feed later TOR deliverables.

---

## 1) TOR literal checks (must be true)

- [ ] The meeting is explicitly described as **TOR 5.3.3 brainstorming meeting**.
- [ ] The meeting purpose explicitly includes **exchange of data, information products, and needs/demand**.
- [ ] The meeting is conducted at least **1 time**.
- [ ] The modality is consistent with TOR: **hotel** or **hybrid/live stream**.
- [ ] Minimum participant count evidenced as **≥80 participants**.

---

## 2) Headcount evidence (what to capture; minimum set)

- [ ] Registration list + sign-in sheet (with organization field)
- [ ] Participant list by organization (cleaned)
- [ ] Breakout room assignment list (who was in which room)
- [ ] Photo set showing plenary + breakout activity
- [ ] Organizer note stating final participant count and method of counting

---

## 3) Organization coverage (practical, not over-specified)

Goal: represent enough of the value chain so exchange is real.

- [ ] Providers present (dataset originators)
- [ ] Integrators/processors present (derived products / modelling / synthesis)
- [ ] Operators/implementers present (implementation/operations)
- [ ] Users/decision makers present (planning/budgeting/policy/response)
- [ ] Governance/rails present (access constraints, standards, interoperability pathways)

Evidence to record:

- [ ] List of organizations by cluster (simple table)
- [ ] Any missing cluster recorded as a limitation in outcomes memo

---

## 4) Agenda evidence: explicit exchange of datasets/products/needs

The agenda must include explicit capture moments that produce structured artifacts.

- [ ] Session includes structured capture of **datasets** (template used)
- [ ] Session includes structured capture of **information products** (template used)
- [ ] Session includes structured capture of **needs/demand** (template used)
- [ ] Outputs are collected per room and consolidated (not “discussion only”)

Recommended minimum artifacts:

- [ ] Baseline capture sheet(s)
- [ ] Needs/demand register
- [ ] Summary synthesis page (what exchanged + what next)

---

## 5) How outputs feed later TOR deliverables (explicit mapping)

- [ ] TOR 5.3.4 (Information Product Inventory): workshop products list becomes candidate inventory entries.
- [ ] TOR 5.3.5 (Baseline Data Inventory): workshop dataset capture becomes candidate baseline inventory entries with required metadata.
- [ ] TOR 5.3.8 (Gap Analysis): workshop needs register + supply constraints inform mismatch mapping.
- [ ] TOR 5.3.9 (Policy/technical recommendations): recurring constraints and minimum touchpoints become recommendation inputs.

Evidence to include:

- [ ] A short “post-workshop assembly note” stating the consolidation steps and where the workshop artifacts are stored.
