# Benchmark — latest foresight report edit vs team-input anchoring concept note

## Scope

- Report benchmark target: [`ψ/lab/foresight-report-wrting/2026-04-02_foresight-2590-integrated-rewrite-v3-edited.md`](ψ/lab/foresight-report-wrting/2026-04-02_foresight-2590-integrated-rewrite-v3-edited.md)
- Benchmark reference: [`plans/2026-04-04_team-input-anchoring-concept-note.md`](plans/2026-04-04_team-input-anchoring-concept-note.md)

## Executive benchmark

Overall assessment: **partially aligned, with strong retention of the team core narrative but noticeable drift in analytical extensions**.

The latest report edit still carries the strongest anchors identified in the concept note:

- children and youth, especially vulnerable groups, remain the narrative center
- violence and security are mostly treated as context rather than the protagonist
- plural learning pathways remain a major structural theme
- trust, safety, and family vulnerability remain central to the explanation of life chances
- the scenario architecture still uses the education x economy logic that the team discussion wanted

However, the report has moved beyond the team-input base in several recurring ways:

- digital is upgraded from a supporting topic into a near-systemic pillar across Chapters 1 to 5
- climate and environmental risk are treated as meaningful report drivers even though the concept note marks them as weakly anchored to team input
- formal foresight method language occupies more narrative space than the concept note recommends
- some strategy and monitoring sections are more technical than the team-anchored narrative requires

## Section-by-section benchmark

### 1. Introduction

**Assessment: mostly aligned**

What still fits the concept note well:

- the introduction keeps the child-centred frame
- the report still argues for a long-horizon view rather than daily conflict narration
- the southern border context is presented as socially and culturally specific, not just conflict-defined

Where drift appears:

- the report gives more space to generalized foresight explanation and system language than the concept note wants
- digital and climate are already introduced as important framing elements, even though the benchmark note treats them as later analytical additions rather than team-origin core narrative

### 2. Signals and system chapter

**Assessment: mixed, leaning aligned in substance but drifting in weighting**

What fits strongly:

- family economy, trust, learning discontinuity, and vulnerable youth trajectories are still central
- the systems logic is consistent with the concept note claim that team inputs already contained a strong implicit systems view

Where drift appears:

- the signal inventory gives digital and climate stronger prominence than the concept note recommends
- the wild cards section relies on analytical expansion rather than clear team-input grounding
- technical foresight framing around signals, loops, uncertainties, and method remains heavier than the concept note’s preferred ownership line

### 3. Scenarios and desirable future

**Assessment: strongest alignment point**

What fits strongly:

- the scenario matrix still uses learning-system pluralism vs centralization and economic opportunity structure as the main axes
- the desirable future remains anchored in dignity, safe environments, connected pathways, and meaningful opportunity
- the narrative still treats cultural identity as a foundation, not an obstacle

Where drift appears:

- digital safety and digital infrastructure are elevated into a standard scenario lens across all worlds
- the desirable future includes more explicit digital-system architecture than the concept note identifies as team-grounded

### 4. Strategy and planning

**Assessment: substantively aligned, rhetorically over-technical**

What fits strongly:

- education pathway reform, income generation, trust, safe space, and community-linked delivery remain the three strongest strategic clusters
- the report still points toward brokers beyond the state alone, which fits the concept note emphasis on civil society, community, and trusted intermediaries

Where drift appears:

- strategy architecture uses strong formal terminology such as backcasting, Three Horizons, leverage points, portfolio categories, robustness testing, and future indicators
- actor mapping is still generic by role and does not yet visibly reflect the more concrete team-anchored actor universe highlighted in the concept note
- digital infrastructure and online safety are treated as full strategic pillars, stronger than the concept note’s benchmark base

### 5. Conclusion and policy implications

**Assessment: aligned on direction, extended on method**

What fits strongly:

- the conclusion still emphasizes cumulative vulnerability, pathway continuity, trust, and human-centred strategy
- the report still argues that fragmented action is insufficient and systemic coordination is needed

Where drift appears:

- policy implications continue the stronger digital and foresight-method framing
- the conclusion reads more like a synthesized policy framework than a team-anchored concept narrative

## Main takeaways

1. The report **has not lost the team core narrative**.
2. The report **has expanded the analytical shell** around that core narrative.
3. The biggest drift is not in the scenario spine or child-centred logic, but in **topic weighting**:
   - digital is now too central relative to the concept note base
   - climate appears more structurally important than the team-input benchmark supports
   - method language is sometimes taking up space that could be used for stronger team-owned framing
4. The strategy chapter is **directionally correct** but needs a stronger visible bridge back to the team’s own actor logic and more practical language.

## Recommended revision priorities

### Priority A — protect the core narrative

- keep human security, child-centred framing, plural education, trust, safe space, and creative economy as the visible backbone
- ensure Chapter 1 and Chapter 5 state this backbone more explicitly than the method language

### Priority B — downgrade or relabel analytical extensions

- keep digital and climate where genuinely useful, but present them as **supporting analytical extensions** rather than as equal-origin team anchors
- reduce repeated wording that makes digital sound like the report’s primary organizing pillar

### Priority C — rebalance Chapter 4

- translate highly technical strategy framing into more team-legible action logic
- anchor actor mapping more concretely to trusted local intermediaries, civil society, community, religious actors, and youth-facing delivery actors

### Priority D — clarify authorship layers

- separate what is:
  - team-origin narrative anchor
  - later analytical synthesis
  - formal foresight method wrapper

### How to use this benchmark

- When revising any section, ask two questions:
  1. does this section still reflect the **team-owned narrative spine**?
  2. does this section actually satisfy the **content job** assigned to it in the output-report reference?

- If a passage is strong in content but misplaced by section function, move or rewrite it.
- If a section uses beautiful prose but does not do its assigned analytical job, treat it as incomplete.

## Cross-cutting writing lens for every revision pass

Use [`plans/foresight-report-writing-style-pack.md`](plans/foresight-report-writing-style-pack.md) as the prose-control companion to this benchmark. In practice:

- this file decides **what the section must do**
- [`plans/foresight-report-writing-style-pack.md`](plans/foresight-report-writing-style-pack.md) decides **how the section should sound and move while doing it**

During revision, apply these cross-cutting style tests to every section:

- write in **Thai-first** prose, not Thai that preserves translated English sentence logic
- keep the analysis **actor-first and child-centred** so readers can see who is acting, who is affected, and whose pathway is narrowing or opening
- make paragraphs **mechanism-first** by showing what drives what, not merely naming themes
- keep **one paragraph, one function** so section roles stay visible
- when writing about learning, frame it as an enabler of **agency, protection, safety, and trust**, not only as formal schooling
- domesticate technical terminology so foresight tools serve the analysis instead of becoming the voice of the report
- keep analytical claims concrete through precise actor naming, lived impacts, and section-appropriate causal logic
